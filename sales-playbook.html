2.1 Website Conversion Optimization Checklist (30 Items)

2.2 Cart Abandonment Recovery (5 Strategies)

Strategy 1: Exit-Intent Popup
Trigger:

Mouse moves toward browser tab/close button (desktop) or 30-second idle + back-button press (mobile).

Offer:

"Wait! Get 10% off your first order" with countdown timer (15 minutes). Collect email in exchange for code.

Implementation:
  1. Install Shopify app: Privy or OptiMonk (free tier sufficient initially)
  2. Design popup matching brand: dark bg, gold accent, product image
  3. A/B test: 10% discount vs free shipping vs free sample sachet
  4. Set frequency cap: show only once per 24 hours per visitor
  5. Track: popup view rate, email capture rate, recovery conversion rate

Expected recovery: 3-5% of abandoning visitors

Strategy 2: Abandoned Cart Email Sequence (3-Email Series)
Timing & Content:
EmailDelaySubject LineContentCTA
Email 11 hour"You left something sweet behind" Product image, cart contents, no discount yet — just a reminder "Complete Your Order"
Email 224 hours"Still thinking? Here's why 2,000+ chose [Brand Name]" Top 3 customer reviews, before/after gut health stats, FAQ "See What Others Say"
Email 372 hours"Last chance: 15% off expires tonight" Urgency-driven, 15% discount code, 24-hour expiry countdown "Claim Your 15% Off"
Implementation:
  1. Set up in Klaviyo or Mailchimp (Shopify-integrated)
  2. Dynamic cart contents block pulling actual abandoned items
  3. Personalize with first name if collected
  4. Exclude customers who already purchased

Expected recovery: 8-12% of abandoned carts across the sequence

Strategy 3: WhatsApp Cart Recovery
Why WhatsApp:

India has 500M+ WhatsApp users. Open rates: 90%+ vs 20% for email. Conversion 3-5x higher than email.

Implementation:
  1. Integrate Wati or Interakt with Shopify (₹2,500-5,000/month)
  2. Trigger automated WhatsApp message 2 hours after cart abandonment
  3. Message template: "Hi [Name]! Your [Brand Name] order is waiting. Complete it now and get free shipping: [Cart Link]"
  4. Follow up at 24 hours with product benefit reminder
  5. Final message at 48 hours with 10% discount code

Expected recovery: 15-25% of abandoned carts reached via WhatsApp

Strategy 4: Retargeting Ads (Meta + Google)
Audience:

Website visitors who added to cart but didn't purchase (7-day window).

Implementation:
  1. Meta Pixel: Create custom audience "Add to Cart — No Purchase" with 7-day window
  2. Google Ads: Dynamic remarketing with product feed
  3. Creative: Carousel of cart items + review quotes + urgency messaging
  4. Budget: ₹200-500/day for retargeting (high ROAS channel)
  5. Frequency cap: 3 impressions per day per person

Expected recovery: 5-8% of retargeted abandoners, ROAS 5-10x

Strategy 5: SMS + Push Notification Recovery
Implementation:
  1. Collect phone numbers at checkout (mandatory for COD, optional for prepaid)
  2. SMS via MSG91 or Gupshup (₹0.15-0.25 per SMS)
  3. Send 1 SMS at 4-hour mark: "Your [Brand Name] cart is saved! Complete order: [short link]"
  4. Web push notification via PushOwl (Shopify app): trigger at 1-hour mark
  5. Track opt-in rates, click rates, and recovery conversion by channel

Expected recovery: 3-5% incremental on top of email/WhatsApp

Combined Cart Recovery Target: With all 5 strategies active, target recovering 25-35% of abandoned carts. At an average cart value of ₹450 and 1,000 monthly abandoned carts, that's ₹1.1L-1.6L in recovered revenue per month.

Why 15% (Not 10% or 20%):

10% feels underwhelming for an unknown brand. 20% trains customers to expect deep discounts and destroys margin. 15% is the proven sweet spot for D2C health brands — meaningful enough to trigger action, sustainable enough to maintain profitability on the first order.

3.1 Seller Central Setup — Food Category

Pre-Requisites for Food Category Approval
Mandatory Documents:
  1. FSSAI License — Central license (not basic registration) required for interstate e-commerce sales. Apply at foscos.fssai.gov.in. Processing time: 30-60 days.
  2. GST Registration — Active GSTIN with HSN code 1702.90 (fructose/FOS products)
  3. Brand Authorization Letter — If selling your own brand, you are the authorized seller
  4. Product Images — Real product photos (not mockups) showing label, ingredients, FSSAI number
  5. Lab Test Reports — NABL-accredited lab reports for nutritional claims
  6. Import Export Code (IEC) — If any raw material is imported
Step-by-Step Approval Process:
  1. Register as Amazon Seller at sellercentral.amazon.in
  2. Complete identity verification (Aadhaar + PAN + bank account)
  3. Navigate to "Add a Product" → search "Grocery & Gourmet Foods"
  4. Click "Request Approval" for the category
  5. Upload: FSSAI license, product images (front + back label), invoice from manufacturer
  6. Submit Seller Declaration Letter with FSSAI details
  7. Wait for approval (typically 24-72 hours, can take up to 2 weeks)
  8. If rejected: address specific feedback, resubmit with corrections
  9. Once approved: create first listing following Grocery & Gourmet Foods Listing Policy

3.4 Amazon Vine Program

What:

Amazon Vine invites trusted reviewers to post opinions about new products. Available to brand-registered sellers. Provides early, high-quality reviews.

Eligibility:
  • Must be enrolled in Amazon Brand Registry
  • Product must have fewer than 30 reviews
  • Must have available FBA inventory
  • Fee: ₹0 enrollment fee (Amazon India waives for first product), ~₹12,000 per parent ASIN for subsequent enrollments
Strategy:
  1. Enroll your hero SKU (250g jar) in Vine immediately upon FBA listing going live
  2. Send up to 30 units for Vine reviewers
  3. Cost of goods: ~30 x ₹250 = ₹7,500 in product cost
  4. Expected reviews: 15-25 reviews within 30 days (not all Vine members review)
  5. These carry "Vine Customer Review of Free Product" badge — trusted by shoppers
  6. Target: Achieve 25+ reviews with 4.0+ average before major ad spend begins

3.8 Buy Box Strategy

Buy Box Win Factors (in order of importance):
  1. Fulfillment method: FBA wins over FBM in almost all cases
  2. Price: Competitive pricing (not necessarily lowest, but within range)
  3. Seller metrics: Order defect rate <1%, late shipment rate <4%, cancellation rate <2.5%
  4. Stock availability: Never go out of stock — Buy Box loss during stockout is extremely costly
  5. Account health: Maintain "Good" or "At Risk" status, never "Critical"

3.10 Returns & Refund Management

Food Category Return Policy:
  • Amazon allows returns on food items only if: product is damaged/defective, wrong item shipped, or past expiry date
  • Quality-related returns (taste complaints): Handle on case-by-case basis — approve refund to maintain seller metrics
  • Target return rate: <3% (food category average is 2-4%)
Return Reduction Tactics:
  1. Clear product description: set accurate expectations about FOS taste (80% sweetness of sugar)
  2. Include "How to Use" insert card in every package
  3. Use tamper-evident packaging to prevent "used and returned" fraud
  4. Respond to negative reviews within 24 hours with helpful, non-defensive responses
  5. Monitor "Voice of Customer" dashboard weekly for early warning signs
