1D. Backend Search Terms — All 200 Bytes

Amazon India Backend Limit: 200 bytes (NOT characters). Standard ASCII = 1 byte per character. Hindi/Devanagari characters = 3 bytes each in UTF-8. Exceeding the limit by even 1 byte causes ALL backend terms to be de-indexed silently.

Strategy: Maximize 200 Bytes

  • Do NOT repeat words already in the title or bullet points (Amazon indexes those automatically)
  • Use spaces between words (no commas, semicolons, or other separators needed)
  • Include misspellings, abbreviations, and alternate spellings that customers actually type
  • Include Hindi transliterations in Roman script (avoids 3-byte Devanagari penalty)
  • Do NOT include brand names of competitors (violates Amazon ToS)

4. BSR (Best Seller Rank) Strategy

4A. How BSR Works

  • Velocity-Based: BSR is calculated based on recent sales velocity relative to other products in the same category. More recent sales are weighted exponentially higher than older sales.
  • Time-Weighted Decay: A sale made in the last hour has ~16x more impact on BSR than a sale made 24 hours ago. BSR updates approximately every 1-2 hours.
  • Category-Specific: A product has a separate BSR for each category and subcategory it is listed in. Optimize for the smallest relevant subcategory first.
  • Not Absolute: BSR #1 in "Sweeteners" subcategory might mean 15-20 sales/day, while BSR #1 in "Grocery" main category might mean 5,000+ sales/day.
  • Organic + Paid: Both organic and PPC-driven sales count toward BSR. This is why launch-phase PPC spending directly improves organic ranking.
