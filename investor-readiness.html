Anti-Dilution Provisions to Negotiate

  • Weighted Average (Broad-Based): Insist on this over Full Ratchet — protects against down rounds while being founder-friendly
  • Pro-Rata Rights: Offer to existing investors in subsequent rounds (standard, builds loyalty)
  • Right of First Refusal (ROFR): Company/existing investors get ROFR on secondary sales
  • Drag-Along: At 75%+ shareholder approval threshold (not 51%)
  • Tag-Along: Standard for all shareholders — protects minorities
  • Liquidation Preference: 1x non-participating preferred (standard) — AVOID >1x or participating preferred
  • Board Seats: Investor board seat only at Seed and above, not at Angel stage

Investor Update Best Practices

  • Send on the same date every month — consistency signals discipline
  • Keep it to 5 minutes — investors read 20+ updates/month. Respect their time.
  • Lead with data, not narrative — numbers first, story second
  • Be honest about challenges — hiding bad news destroys trust. Share problems + your plan.
  • Make "How You Can Help" specific — "intro to X person" beats "share with your network"
  • Include a forward-looking element — what's the #1 goal for next month?
  • CC all investors — angels, VCs, advisors. Build community. They refer each other.
  • Send even when there's no new funding — updates to potential investors build relationship for when you ARE raising
  • Use a simple email format — no fancy HTML. Plain text or minimal formatting. Works on mobile.
