01 · Context
The Opportunity
Revelations Biotech manufactures sc-FOS — a prebiotic fiber that tastes like sugar, feeds gut bacteria, and costs 28.6% less than the nearest branded competitor. No consumer brand exists for it in India. This document covers four decisions needed before anything else moves: what to name the brand, what the packaging looks like, what the identity system is, and the full ₹10Cr investment to build it.

02 · Brand Naming
10 Name Candidates
Each name was evaluated for global viability — these must work in India, UAE, US, UK, and Southeast Asia from Day 1. No Indian-language names. Every candidate is built for international expansion. Scoring: memorability (recall after single exposure), meaning (does it evoke sweetness, health, naturalness?), global fluency (works in English, Hindi, Arabic, Malay — no awkward translations), domain availability (.com/.in), trademark clearance (India Class 30 + Class 5, plus Madrid Protocol pre-check), and shelf presence (stands out globally). Tier 1 — Top Picks

These three names scored highest across all criteria. Built for global scale from Day 1.

Recommend

BLOOM

/bloom/
When you feed your gut flora, they bloom. Growth, vitality, flourishing health. One word that captures the entire prebiotic promise.

Why it works: BLOOM is the name that sells the benefit without saying a word about science. Your gut flora literally blooms when you consume FOS — the name IS the product story. It works in a Mumbai supermarket ("Try Bloom") and a Whole Foods in Manhattan ("I use Bloom"). Five letters. One syllable. The word carries universally positive associations in every culture — flowers opening, spring arriving, health glowing. On a gold hexagonal jar, "BLOOM" in Playfair Display serif is warm, premium, and inviting. It doesn't compete with clinical names like "Sugar Free" or "Equal" — it exists in a completely different emotional register. Portfolio expansion is effortless: Bloom Sweet, Bloom Fiber Bars, Bloom Prebiotic Soda, Bloom Drops. Every line extension makes the brand stronger because "Bloom" is a platform, not a product name.

Tagline pairing: "BLOOM — Feed What Grows." / "BLOOM — Sweetness That Blooms."

Global test: No negative meaning in any major language. Works in Arabic ("bloom" is neutral/positive), Malay, Mandarin contexts. Already understood as a lifestyle/wellness word worldwide. Bloom Nutrition (US supplements) exists in a different category — no Class 30 food conflict.

Shelf test: Green Sugar Free, blue Equal, white stevia packets — then a gold jar that says BLOOM. It's the only name on the shelf that makes you feel something.

5C — CRO Strategy & Optimisation Framework

Conversion Rate Optimisation is not a one-time setup — it is a continuous process built into the site from Day 1. The CRO framework operates on a 2-week sprint cycle: identify friction → hypothesise → test → implement winner → repeat.

Above the Fold

First-Screen Conversion Triggers

Homepage: Hero headline with value prop + hero SKU image + single CTA ("Shop Now" or "Discover the Science") + trust strip (FSSAI, plant-based, 10,000+ customers). No slider/carousel — static hero with A/B tested headline variants. Mobile: hero image → headline → CTA → trust badges, all within first viewport scroll.
PDP: Product image (left 50%) + title, price, variant selector, Add to Cart, trust badges (right 50%) all visible without scrolling on desktop. Mobile: image → sticky price bar → scroll for details.

Trust Architecture

Trust Signals Hierarchy

Level 1 (Every page): FSSAI licence number in footer, SSL badge, secure payment icons, "100% Plant-Based" badge.
Level 2 (Product pages): Star ratings, review count, "X sold this week" social proof, lab test report download link, ingredient transparency panel.
Level 3 (Checkout): Money-back guarantee badge, estimated delivery date, secure checkout lock icon, COD availability note, customer support WhatsApp number.
Level 4 (Post-purchase): Order tracking, delivery confirmation with feedback request, 30-day satisfaction guarantee reminder.

Friction Reduction

Checkout Optimisation

Guest checkout default — email capture only, account creation offered post-purchase. Address autofill via Google Places API + saved addresses for returning users. Payment stack: UPI (PhonePe, GPay, Paytm) prioritised → Credit/Debit cards → Net Banking → COD (₹30 fee to discourage, shown as "Free with prepaid"). Cart persistence: 30-day localStorage + server-side for logged-in users. Abandonment recovery: Email at 30 min, WhatsApp at 2 hours (with consent), retargeting ad at 24 hours.

Micro-Conversions

Progressive Engagement Ladder

Step 1: Newsletter signup (10% discount hook) — captures email before purchase intent. Step 2: Quiz completion ("Find Your Perfect Format") — captures preferences + recommends SKU. Step 3: Free sample request (pay ₹49 shipping) — converts sceptics with zero-risk trial. Step 4: First purchase — welcome email sequence (5 emails over 14 days). Step 5: Subscription conversion — triggered at Day 25 ("Running low? Subscribe and never run out"). Step 6: Referral program activation — triggered after 2nd purchase.

5E — Tech Stack & Performance Targets

The stack is selected for speed, SEO, and D2C conversion — not engineering elegance. Every choice is justified by its impact on Core Web Vitals and conversion rate.

Platform

Shopify Plus (India)

Why Shopify: Fastest time-to-market (4 weeks to live store), native Indian payment gateway integrations (Razorpay, Cashfree), COD support out-of-box, subscription support via Recharge/Loop, app ecosystem for reviews (Judge.me), loyalty (Smile.io), and analytics (Triple Whale). Why Plus: Checkout customisation (Shopify Scripts for bundle discounts), higher API rate limits for inventory sync with Revelations warehouse, multi-currency readiness for international expansion.
Monthly cost: ~$2,300/mo ($27,600/yr). Included in Engine B digital budget.

Frontend

Custom Theme (Liquid + Tailwind)

No off-the-shelf theme. Custom Liquid theme built on Shopify's Dawn as a base, stripped to essentials and rebuilt with Tailwind CSS. Reasons: control over every pixel for CRO, eliminate theme bloat (typical themes load 500KB+ of unused CSS/JS), custom product comparison components, animated science page. Image optimisation: All product images served as WebP via Shopify CDN with lazy loading. Hero images preloaded. No images above 100KB.
Font loading: Playfair Display + Inter self-hosted via @font-face with font-display: swap. Subset to Latin + Devanagari glyphs.

Analytics & Tracking

Full-Stack Measurement

GA4 (server-side via GTM Server Container on Cloud Run) for privacy-compliant, ad-blocker-resistant tracking. Event taxonomy: view_item, add_to_cart, begin_checkout, purchase, subscription_start, referral_share — all with product-level parameters (SKU, format, price, discount_code). Meta Pixel + CAPI for Facebook/Instagram ad optimisation. Google Ads conversion tracking with enhanced conversions. Hotjar/Microsoft Clarity for heatmaps, session recordings, and rage-click detection. Looker Studio dashboard with daily automated email: sessions, conversion rate, AOV, top products, funnel drop-offs.

Performance

Core Web Vitals Targets

LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): <2.5s on 4G mobile. Achieved via: preloaded hero image, critical CSS inlined, Shopify CDN with India edge nodes. FID/INP (Interaction to Next Paint): <200ms. Achieved via: minimal JS (target <80KB gzipped), deferred non-critical scripts, no render-blocking third-party widgets. CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): <0.1. Achieved via: explicit image dimensions, font-display: swap with size-adjust, no dynamically injected banners above the fold. TTFB: <600ms via Shopify's global CDN + India-region hosting. Lighthouse score target: >90 on Performance, Accessibility, Best Practices, SEO.

5F — Mobile-First UX Architecture
