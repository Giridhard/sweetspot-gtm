01 · Context
The Opportunity
Revelations Biotech manufactures sc-FOS — a prebiotic fiber that tastes like sugar, feeds gut bacteria, and costs 28.6% less than the nearest branded competitor. No consumer brand exists for it yet. This document covers three decisions needed before anything else moves: what to name the brand, what it should look and feel like, and what the initial investment is to bring it to life.

02 · Brand Naming
Naming Strategy
Brand name is the single most consequential decision before anything else moves. The naming process follows a structured methodology designed for the Indian FMCG sweetener category.

Naming Criteria Framework

CriterionWhat It MeasuresWeight
MemorabilityCan someone recall the name after hearing it once? Does it stick? Is it easy to spell?20%
Meaning & RelevanceDoes the name communicate sweetness, gut health, or natural origin?20%
Bilingual FluencyDoes it work in both Hindi and English? Can a shopkeeper in Tier 2 say it comfortably?15%
Domain & Handle AvailabilityIs .com / .in available or acquirable? Are social handles clean?15%
Trademark ClearanceRisk of conflicts in Class 30 (food additives) and Class 5 (dietary supplements) in India15%
Shelf PresenceWill it stand out on a retail shelf next to Sugar Free, Tata NQ, and stevia brands?15%
Phase 1 · Week 1

Discovery & Long-List

Workshop with Revelations leadership to define brand territory, emotional positioning, and naming guardrails. Generate 50+ candidate names across 3 directions: Indian Heritage, Modern Global, and Science-Meets-Lifestyle. Narrow to 12 via internal scoring.

Phase 2 · Week 2

Research & Short-List

Consumer pulse survey (200 respondents, 4 metros) testing top 12 names for recall, likability, and category association. Preliminary TM search (India Class 30 + Class 5). Narrow to top 3 finalists with data backing.

Phase 3 · Week 3

Lock & Protect

Comprehensive TM search on top 3 (₹15K, 5-day turnaround). Domain acquisition (.com, .in, .co.in, .health). Social handle reservation across Instagram, Twitter, YouTube, Facebook. TM filing. Final name selected.

Typography Direction

Headlines

Playfair Display

Serif. Elegant, editorial, premium. Signals heritage and credibility. Used for headlines, hero sections, and packaging feature text. Communicates "this is not a cheap substitute."

Body & UI

Inter

Clean sans-serif. Optimized for screens. Used for body text, product descriptions, website UI, and all digital surfaces. Highly readable at small sizes — critical for nutritional info and packaging fine print.

Data & Labels

JetBrains Mono

Monospace. For nutritional data, lab results, clinical numbers, and metadata. Signals scientific precision and transparency. "We show the numbers because we're proud of them."

Approve the 3-phase naming process (Discovery → Research → Lock). Inspire Labs runs the full naming sprint in Week 1–3 of Engine A — workshop, consumer testing, TM search, domain acquisition, and final selection.

Approve or refine the visual direction — color palette, typography, packaging concept. This becomes the brief for the design team. Refinements are expected; this is a starting point, not a final.

Engine A Go/No-Go

Approve the ₹1.40Cr initial investment to begin brand foundation. Capital deployment: ₹40L at signing, ₹50L at Week 4 (design phase), ₹50L at Week 10 (production phase).
