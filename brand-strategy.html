Brand Essence Statement

The Soul of the Brand in 3-5 Words

Sweetness That Heals

Every interaction, product, and message must embody this truth: [Brand Name] is the only sweetener that actively makes your body better with every spoonful. Not neutral. Not "less bad." Actually healing.

Brand Purpose

Why We Exist Beyond Profit

We exist to liberate India from the false choice between sweetness and health. For too long, 237 million diabetics and hundreds of millions of health-conscious families have been told to choose: enjoy the sweetness that makes life beautiful, or protect your body. We reject that choice. Through the science of prebiotics and the magic of enzymatic transformation, we have created a sweetener that does what no other can — it makes your gut stronger, your blood sugar stable, and your taste buds happy, all at the same time. We exist so that every cup of chai, every bowl of kheer, every celebration mithai can be an act of self-care, not self-harm.

Brand Vision

Where We Are Going

To become the world’s most trusted functional sweetener brand — present in every Indian kitchen by 2032 and in 50 countries by 2035 — making prebiotic sweetness the global default and rendering artificial sweeteners obsolete.

Brand Mission

How We Get There

We will achieve our vision through five strategic imperatives:

  • Science-First Innovation: Continuously advance FOS technology through our patented enzymatic process, expanding into new functional food formats — energy drinks, protein bars, non-alcoholic beverages — while maintaining the highest purity standards.
  • Radical Accessibility: Maintain the most aggressive pricing in the natural sweetener category (currently 44% below the nearest competitor) so that prebiotic sweetness is never a privilege reserved for the affluent.
  • Education Over Advertising: Invest in consumer education about gut health, prebiotics, and the glycemic index, building a generation of informed eaters who choose function over marketing.
  • Omnichannel Ubiquity: Be present wherever sweetness is consumed — from Amazon to the corner kirana, from five-star hotel kitchens to hospital canteens, from quick commerce to international supermarkets.
  • Community of Care: Build a community of health advocates — doctors, nutritionists, fitness coaches, mothers, and diabetics — who become ambassadors not because we pay them, but because the product genuinely changed their lives.

Brand Values

Seven core values that guide every decision, from product formulation to customer service to investor relations. Each value includes behavioral definitions so they remain actionable, not decorative.

1. Radical Honesty

What This Means in Action

We never hide behind jargon, small print, or misleading health claims. If our product has a limitation (e.g., slight bloating at high doses for first-time users), we say so on the pack. We publish our full ingredient list, sourcing origins, and lab test results. We never use "natural flavoring" as a catch-all. Every claim on our packaging has a citation. We would rather lose a sale than gain a customer through deception.

2. Science as Compass

What This Means in Action

Every product decision is rooted in peer-reviewed research, not trend-chasing. We invest 8% of revenue in R&D. We collaborate with ICMR, CSIR-CFTRI, and university labs. We do not launch new SKUs based on marketing hypotheses — we require clinical or analytical validation. We share our research openly and invite independent verification. When the science says no, the answer is no, regardless of the commercial opportunity.

3. Democratic Health

What This Means in Action

Healthy sweetening should not be a luxury. We price aggressively (currently £0.48/g, 44% below Sugar Free Green) and will fight to maintain this advantage. We offer sachet formats at £2 price points so that autorickshaw drivers and chai wallahs can afford prebiotics. We refuse to position as "premium" if it means excluding the majority of India. We measure success not by revenue per customer, but by the number of households we reach.

4. Gut-First Thinking

What This Means in Action

The gut microbiome is our North Star. Every product extension — whether an energy drink, a protein bar, or a baking mix — must demonstrably support gut health. We never add an ingredient that compromises prebiotic function for taste or shelf life. Our R&D team includes microbiologists, not just food technologists. We track gut health outcomes in our consumer research, not just purchase intent.

5. Cultural Reverence

What This Means in Action

We honor India's deep, joyful relationship with sweetness. We never shame sugar consumption — we offer a better alternative. Our recipes celebrate mithai, payasam, laddu, and jalebi. Our marketing features real Indian families, not aspirational Western lifestyles. We develop products for Indian cooking methods (high heat tolerance, syrup consistency for Bengali sweets). We are proudly Made in Hyderabad, and we lead with our Indian identity in global markets.

6. Obsessive Quality

What This Means in Action

Our FOS purity exceeds 95%. Every batch is tested for heavy metals, pesticide residue, and microbial contamination. We hold ISO 22000, FSSAI, HALAL, and Kosher certifications. Our factory in Cherlapally, Hyderabad operates under pharmaceutical-grade GMP standards. We reject batches that pass industry standards but fail our internal benchmarks. Quality is never a cost to be optimized — it is the product.

7. Courageous Patience

What This Means in Action

Category creation takes time. We will not sacrifice long-term brand equity for short-term revenue targets. We will invest in education before demand exists. We will build relationships with doctors over years, not months. We will say no to white-label deals that dilute our brand even when they offer immediate revenue. We measure in decades, not quarters. We are building a generational brand, not a quick exit.

Brand Manifesto

We Believe in a Sweeter Truth.

For centuries, sweetness has been woven into the fabric of Indian life. The laddu at a naming ceremony. The payasam at Onam. The mithai box exchanged at Diwali. The two spoons of sugar in the chai that fuels a nation of dreamers. Sweetness is not a vice. It is a language — of celebration, of love, of belonging.

But somewhere along the way, sweetness became the enemy. Doctors warned against it. Labels shouted "sugar-free." Guilt crept into every bite of gulab jamun. And the alternatives? Chemical compounds born in laboratories, with names no grandmother could pronounce. Sweeteners that tricked the tongue but did nothing for the body. Or worse — sweeteners that quietly damaged the very gut bacteria that keep us healthy.

We refused to accept this. We are scientists, yes. But we are also Indians who grew up stealing jaggery from the kitchen, who associate sweetness with every good memory we have. So we asked a different question: What if a sweetener could actually heal?

Not "less harmful." Not "zero calorie." But genuinely, measurably healing. A sweetener that feeds the good bacteria in your gut. That keeps your blood sugar steady. That tastes like the sugar you love because it is made from sugar — transformed through enzymes, not chemistry, into something extraordinary.

That is [Brand Name]. Born in Hyderabad. Backed by patents. Proven by science. And priced so that every family in India — not just the affluent few — can make the switch.

We are not here to tell you to stop enjoying sweetness. We are here to make sweetness worthy of you. To turn every cup of chai into a prebiotic dose. To make every celebration mithai an act of self-care. To prove that in the 21st century, the best things in life are not just free — they are functional.

This is not a sugar substitute. This is a sugar evolution. And it starts with the next spoonful.

Brand Personality Do's and Don'ts

10 DO's
  1. Do speak like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook or a salesperson
  2. Do celebrate Indian food culture — feature mithai, chai, regional sweets in every campaign
  3. Do cite specific evidence ("FOS feeds Bifidobacterium, increasing good gut bacteria by up to 10x")
  4. Do show real families — diverse body types, ages, skin tones, regions, and economic backgrounds
  5. Do use warm, earthy visuals — golden hues, kitchen settings, natural textures
  6. Do acknowledge the emotional relationship Indians have with sugar — never dismiss it
  7. Do use humor gently — warm smiles, not sarcasm or shock value
  8. Do lead with benefits first, then explain the science for those who want to go deeper
  9. Do share customer stories with permission — real testimonials from real people with real names
  10. Do always include a recipe or usage tip — every touchpoint should teach something practical
10 DON'Ts
  1. Don't shame sugar consumption — never say "sugar is poison" or use fear-based messaging
  2. Don't use clinical or cold language — avoid "dietary supplement," "therapeutic dose," or "glycemic management protocol"
  3. Don't feature gym-perfect bodies as the aspirational ideal — health is holistic, not aesthetic
  4. Don't use Western lifestyle imagery — no blonde families, no quinoa bowls, no CrossFit boxes
  5. Don't make medical claims — never say "cures diabetes" or "prevents cancer." Use "supports," "helps," "contributes to"
  6. Don't position as premium or exclusive — never imply that health-conscious living is for the wealthy
  7. Don't use trendy slang that will age poorly — no "slay," "lit," "vibe check" in official brand comms
  8. Don't attack competitors by name — focus on category distinction ("unlike artificial sweeteners") not brand attacks
  9. Don't overpromise — never claim 1:1 sugar taste replacement if it is 70% sweetness. Be precise.
  10. Don't ignore the aftertaste question — address it proactively and honestly (FOS has no bitter aftertaste unlike stevia)

Brand Crisis Playbook

Five scenarios with structured response frameworks. Each follows the STAR protocol: Stop, Triage, Act, Recover.

Crisis 1: Product Quality Issue / Recall

Scenario: A batch of [Brand Name] is found to contain contaminants, off-taste, or fails quality standards post-distribution.

Stop (0-2 hours): Halt all sales of affected batch. Notify marketplace partners (Amazon, Flipkart, BigBasket) to pause listings. Alert customer service team.

Triage (2-6 hours): Identify scope (batch number, distribution footprint, units sold). Get R&D lab analysis. Assess health risk (consultation with food safety advisor). Determine voluntary vs. mandatory recall.

Act (6-24 hours): Issue public statement (social media, website, email to all customers): admit the issue, explain what happened, detail corrective action, offer full refund + replacement. Use CBO as spokesperson. Tone: Caregiver (deeply concerned) + Sage (transparent about facts). Never deflect or minimize.

Recover (1-4 weeks): Publish root cause analysis. Announce process improvements. Offer "quality assurance" discount code for returning customers. Share enhanced testing protocol publicly. Commission independent third-party audit and publish results.

Forbidden: Never blame suppliers. Never say "our product is safe" without lab evidence. Never delete negative social media comments.

Crisis 2: Social Media Backlash / Viral Negative Post

Scenario: An influencer, customer, or media outlet posts negative content (health concern, taste complaint, misleading claim accusation) that goes viral.

Stop: Do not react impulsively. Do not delete comments. Do not engage trolls. Internal team meeting within 2 hours.

Triage: Assess validity of the complaint. Is it factually accurate? Partially accurate? Entirely false? Determine the scale (likes, shares, media pickup).

Act: If valid: Acknowledge publicly, thank the poster, explain corrective action. If partially valid: Acknowledge the valid part, provide evidence for the contested part. If false: Respond with facts, linked evidence, and a calm invitation to verify. Always offer a direct conversation (DM or call).

Recover: Produce content addressing the concern proactively (FAQ, video, infographic). If the issue was genuine, feature the improvement story. Reach out to neutral third-party voices (doctors, nutritionists) for independent commentary.

Crisis 3: Competitor Attack Campaign

Scenario: Sugar Free or another competitor runs a campaign implying [Brand Name] is unsafe, unproven, or misleading.

Stop: Do not counter-attack. Do not name the competitor in any public response.

Triage: Document the competitor's claims precisely. Have legal counsel assess for defamation or comparative advertising violations. Prepare factual rebuttals.

Act: Respond with a "positive reaffirmation" campaign: double down on our science, publish additional evidence, accelerate doctor endorsement program. If the competitor's claims are legally actionable, issue a cease-and-desist through legal channels (not public). Use the moment to educate about "prebiotic sweetener" category — turn their attack into our category-building opportunity.

Recover: Use the increased attention to amplify brand awareness. Feature the controversy in PR pitches (without naming competitors). "Controversy" creates attention; redirect it to education.

Crisis 4: Regulatory Challenge

Scenario: FSSAI questions a health claim, issues a warning, or introduces new regulations that affect FOS product labeling or marketing.

Stop: Immediately cease any communication using the contested claim. Notify all sales channels and agency partners.

Triage: Engage regulatory affairs consultant. Assess whether the issue is labeling, health claim wording, or product classification. Determine timeline for compliance.

Act: Full cooperation with regulatory authority. Proactive compliance (exceed requirements, not just meet them). Revise labeling and marketing materials within regulatory timelines. Communicate to customers: "We take regulatory compliance seriously. We have updated our [X] to reflect the latest guidelines."

Recover: Use the compliance moment to demonstrate the Radical Honesty value. Publish a blog post: "What the new FSSAI guidelines mean for prebiotic sweeteners." Position as the responsible industry leader who welcomes regulation.

Crisis 5: Founder Controversy

Scenario: A Revelations Biotech founder or senior executive is involved in a personal controversy (social media statement, legal issue, ethical allegation) that reflects on the brand.

Stop: Separate the individual's personal actions from the brand immediately. No public defense of the individual using brand channels.

Triage: Assess the severity and relevance to brand values. Is the controversy related to the product or industry? Does it violate stated brand values? Is it a personal matter that the brand should not comment on?

Act: If related to brand values: Board-level response. The brand distances itself from the behavior (not the person) and reaffirms its values. If unrelated personal matter: Brief statement that personal matters are separate from brand operations, and the brand's commitment to its values remains unchanged. In severe cases: Consider whether the individual should step back from brand-facing roles during resolution.

Recover: Reinforce that [Brand Name] is bigger than any individual. Emphasize the science, the product, and the mission. Shift brand storytelling toward the team, the factory, and the community rather than founder narrative.
