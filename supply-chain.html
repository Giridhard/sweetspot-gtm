FOS Production Methods

Enzymatic Transfructosylation (from Sucrose)
Primary Method

Uses fructosyltransferase enzyme (from Aspergillus niger) to convert sucrose into short-chain FOS (GF2, GF3, GF4). Produces scFOS with 95% purity. Used by Meiji, Ingredion, Quantum Hi-Tech, Baolingbao, and Revelations Biotech.

Chicory Root Extraction (Hydrolysis of Inulin)
European Method

Hot water extraction of inulin from chicory roots, followed by partial enzymatic hydrolysis to produce oligofructose (FOS). Used by BENEO, Cosucra, Sensus, Cargill. Produces longer-chain FOS with different prebiotic profile.

Currency Risk Factors

USD/INR Volatility

INR has depreciated ~3-5% annually against USD over the past decade. For import-dependent supply chains, this adds 3-5% annual cost inflation. Hedging costs an additional 2-3%.

CNY/INR Risk

Chinese FOS is often quoted in USD but settled in CNY. Double currency conversion adds hidden costs. Direct INR-CNY settlement is now available through some banks.

EUR/INR for European Sourcing

European suppliers (BENEO, Cosucra) price in EUR. EUR/INR volatility typically 5-8% annually. Long-term contracts with fixed pricing help mitigate.

Sustainable Sourcing

FOS from Enzymatic Process
Low Environmental Impact

FOS produced via enzymatic transfructosylation from sucrose is inherently a green process -- uses biological catalysts (enzymes), operates at moderate temperatures, generates minimal waste, and the primary byproduct (glucose) is fully recoverable.

Domestic Sourcing Advantage
Reduced Carbon Footprint

Sourcing FOS domestically from Revelations Biotech (Hyderabad) vs. importing from China/Europe eliminates: international shipping emissions (sea freight), customs processing, and multi-stage handling. Estimated carbon savings: 0.5-1.2 kg CO2 per kg of FOS vs. imported.

Sugarcane-Based Supply Chain
Indian Agriculture Support

FOS from sucrose enzymatic process uses Indian sugarcane as the ultimate feedstock, supporting Indian farmers and the domestic sugar industry. This creates a compelling "Make in India" narrative.
