01 — Amazon A9/COSMO Algorithm Deep-Dive

1.1 How Amazon's Search Algorithm Ranks Products

Amazon's product search engine has evolved from a simple keyword-matching system (A9) into a multi-layered, AI-driven ranking engine. The goal remains the same: maximize revenue per search query by showing products most likely to convert. Understanding this system is the foundation of every optimization decision in this document.

1.2 The A9 Foundation — Still Active in 2026

A9 is the core indexing and retrieval system. When a shopper types a query, A9 determines which products are eligible to appear (the "candidate pool"). Indexing is binary — you're either in the pool or you're not. Keywords in your title, bullet points, description, and backend search terms determine indexing eligibility.

Critical Insight: A9 handles the first gate — whether your product is even considered. If your listing doesn't contain the exact keyword (or a close semantic variant), you won't enter the candidate pool regardless of how good your product is. This is why backend search terms and keyword coverage remain essential even in the COSMO era.

1.3 COSMO — Amazon's AI Knowledge Graph (Deployed 2024-2026)

COSMO (Common Sense Knowledge for E-commerce) is Amazon's semantic knowledge engine layered on top of A9. It is NOT a replacement for A9 — it's an AI layer that re-ranks products within the candidate pool based on intent understanding rather than keyword matching.

How COSMO Differs from A9

A9 (Keyword Matching)

  • Matches exact keywords in listing text
  • Binary indexing: yes/no
  • Keyword density influences relevance
  • Optimized by stuffing keywords
  • Static ranking based on text match
  • Title carries heaviest keyword weight

COSMO (Intent Understanding)

  • Interprets shopper intent from queries
  • Contextual relevance scoring
  • Listing accuracy influences relevance
  • Optimized by describing use cases truthfully
  • Dynamic ranking based on behavior data
  • Discovery attributes feed directly into COSMO

2.2 Title Construction Rules (2026 Compliance)

  • No word may appear more than twice (except articles, conjunctions, prepositions)
  • No promotional phrases: "Best seller," "#1," "Top rated," "Hot deal," "Free shipping"

  • Spell out numbers under 10 (except measurements like "250g")
  • Include the brand name first
  • No subjective claims: "Amazing," "Premium quality," "Best tasting"

04 — Backend Search Terms (250 Bytes)

4.1 Backend Search Terms Strategy

Backend search terms are hidden keywords that shoppers never see but Amazon uses for indexing. You get 250 bytes (not characters — multi-byte characters like Hindi use more bytes). The strategy is to include every relevant keyword NOT already in your title, bullets, or description. Never repeat words already used in visible listing content.

Rules for Backend Search Terms (2026):
  • 249-byte hard limit — exceeding even by 1 byte means Amazon ignores ALL your backend terms
  • No commas needed — spaces separate keywords (commas waste bytes)
  • No brand name repetition (already in title)
  • No ASINs or competitor brand names in backend (policy violation risk since 2024 update)
  • Include misspellings, Hindi transliterations, and synonyms
  • Singular forms only (Amazon auto-stems plurals)
  • No subjective claims ("best," "premium," "top rated")
  • No temporary statements ("new," "on sale")

12 — Flipkart Optimization

12.1 How Flipkart Search Differs from Amazon

Flipkart and Amazon have fundamentally different search algorithms. While Amazon uses A9/COSMO with heavy emphasis on sales velocity and conversion rate, Flipkart uses its own algorithm that weights Listing Quality Score (LQS), seller performance metrics, competitive pricing, and delivery speed differently.

Amazon A9/COSMO

  • Sales velocity is #1 ranking factor
  • Backend search terms (250 bytes)
  • A+ Content boosts conversion
  • FBA gives ranking advantage
  • COSMO interprets intent via knowledge graph
  • Brand Registry unlocks features
  • Review quality weighted heavily

Flipkart Algorithm (2026)

  • Listing Quality Score (LQS) is critical
  • No backend search terms — all keywords must be visible
  • Product Content Score drives visibility
  • Flipkart Assured badge matters
  • Price competitiveness weighted more heavily
  • Seller performance (cancellation rate, delivery speed)
  • Flipkart Plus SuperCoins influence discovery
