Budget Scaling Methodology

Scaling Rules

  1. 20% Rule: Never increase daily budget by more than 20% per change. Larger jumps reset the learning phase.
  2. Wait for 50 conversions: Don't touch a campaign until it exits the learning phase (50 conversion events per week).
  3. CBO vs ABO: Start with Ad Set Budget Optimization (ABO) to control spend per audience. Switch to Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO) once you have 3+ winning ad sets.
  4. Horizontal Scaling: Duplicate winning ad sets into new campaigns targeting new LAL audiences or interest stacks. This prevents audience fatigue.
  5. Vertical Scaling: Increase budget on proven ad sets by 15-20% every 3-4 days if ROAS holds.
  6. Kill Criteria: Pause any ad set that hasn't converted after spending 2x target CAC. Pause any ad with frequency above 4.0 in a 7-day window.

Keyword Harvesting Workflow

Phase 1: Auto Campaigns (Week 1-4)

Launch Sponsored Products auto campaigns with ₹500/day budget. Let Amazon's algorithm discover converting keywords. Set up 4 match types: Close Match, Loose Match, Substitutes, Complements.

Phase 2: Search Term Harvesting (Week 2-4)

Download Search Term Reports weekly. Identify keywords with 3+ orders and ACoS below 30%. These become exact match keywords in manual campaigns. Keywords with high spend and zero conversions become negative keywords.

Phase 3: Manual Campaign Build (Week 3-5)

Create manual campaigns: Exact Match (harvested winners), Phrase Match (top 20 category terms), Broad Match (discovery — low bids). Set exact match bids 20-30% higher than auto to win placement.

Phase 4: ASIN Targeting (Week 4-6)

Target competitor product pages: Sugar Free, Stevia drops, other sweetener ASINs. Also target complementary products: protein powder, green tea, diabetic care products.

Phase 5: Optimization Loop (Ongoing)

Weekly: Add new converting terms from auto to manual. Add non-converting terms as negatives. Adjust bids based on ACoS. Monthly: Review placement performance (Top of Search vs Rest of Search vs Product Pages).

A/B Testing Methodology

Testing Hierarchy (Test in This Order)

  1. Hook (first 3 seconds): The #1 variable. Test 3-5 hooks per creative concept. 70% of ad performance is determined here.
  2. Creative Format: Video vs static vs carousel. UGC vs polished. Talking head vs hands-only.
  3. Offer: 10% off vs free shipping vs bundle deal. Test price anchoring.
  4. CTA: "Shop Now" vs "Learn More" vs "Get Yours" vs "Try Free." Different CTAs for different funnel stages.
  5. Audience: Interest stacking combinations. LAL percentages. Age/geo splits.
  6. Headline: Problem-led vs benefit-led vs price-led vs curiosity-led.

Testing Rules

  • Change ONE variable at a time
  • Minimum budget per variant: ₹500-1,000 or 1,000 impressions
  • Run test for at least 3-5 days (avoid day-of-week bias)
  • Need 100+ clicks per variant for statistically significant CTR test
  • Need 30+ conversions per variant for conversion rate test
  • Winner = variant with best Cost Per Purchase (not just CTR)
  • Archive all test results in a Creative Testing Log
  • Kill losers fast (after 2x CAC spend with no conversion)
  • Scale winners by duplicating into new ad sets
  • Creative fatigue: refresh winning creatives every 3-4 weeks

Incrementality Testing

Incrementality testing answers the hardest question: "Would this sale have happened without the ad?" Run quarterly tests to validate channel contribution.

Geo-Based Lift Test

  1. Select 2 similar city pairs (e.g., Pune vs Ahmedabad)
  2. Run ads in City A (test), dark in City B (control)
  3. Measure organic + paid sales in both cities for 2-4 weeks
  4. Incremental lift = (Test sales - Control sales) / Control sales
  5. Calculate true iROAS (incremental ROAS)

Holdout Testing

  1. Randomly split retargeting audience: 90% see ads, 10% holdout
  2. Measure purchase rate in both groups
  3. If holdout group still buys at 80% the rate, retargeting is only 20% incremental
  4. Adjust retargeting budget accordingly
  5. Run monthly for each retargeting campaign

  • "vs Sugar Free" comparison ads: Conquest marketing works because Sugar Free is the default choice. Showing the ingredient difference converts 15-20% of viewers who click.
  • WhatsApp click-to-chat: Bypasses landing page friction entirely. 15-25% of chat initiators convert to purchase. Best performing bottom-funnel channel.
  • Retargeting abandoned carts: 60-70% of carts are abandoned. A WhatsApp + Meta retargeting combo recovers 8-15% of abandoned carts.
  • Festive season campaigns: Diwali, Navratri, and New Year health resolutions drive 3-5x normal volumes. Prepare creatives and budgets 30 days in advance.
  • Recipe content ads: Sugar-free dessert recipes perform like entertainment content with commercial intent. CPMs are 30-50% lower than direct product ads.
  • FOS-specific keyword targeting: CPC arbitrage — ₹1-5 vs ₹8-15 for generic terms. Category keywords deliver 2-3x better ROAS than generic terms.
  • Subscribe & Save on Amazon: Builds recurring revenue and reduces CAC over time. Target 20-30% S&S adoption within 6 months.
  • UGC-style creatives: Outperform polished studio content by 1.5-2x on Meta. Authentic, phone-shot videos feel trustworthy.

WHAT DOESN'T WORK

  • Generic "buy now" display ads: Cold display with generic creatives wastes 80%+ of budget. Display should be for remarketing only.
  • Broad targeting without exclusions: Running broad interest targeting without excluding existing customers leads to wasted spend on people who've already bought.
  • Ignoring negative keywords: On Amazon and Google, not actively managing negatives can waste 20-30% of search spend on irrelevant terms.
  • Not testing creatives: Running the same 2-3 ads for months leads to creative fatigue. Refresh every 3-4 weeks. Test 5-10 new creatives monthly.
  • Spending equally across channels: Allocating budget equally ignores where your customers actually convert. Follow the data — some channels will 4x outperform others.
  • Scaling too fast without unit economics: Increasing budget 2-3x overnight crashes ROAS. Follow the 20% rule. Only scale when CAC is profitable at current spend.
  • Over-indexing on vanity metrics: Likes, followers, impressions don't pay bills. Optimize for purchases and CAC. A post with 5 likes and 3 sales beats 5,000 likes and 0 sales.
  • Ignoring post-purchase experience: No follow-up, no review request, no reorder nudge = wasting the hardest part (acquisition) by not building LTV.
  • Running only bottom-funnel: Pure BOF campaigns work for 2-3 months, then audiences exhaust. You need TOF to constantly fill the funnel.
  • Copying competitor creative verbatim: India's ad library is transparent. Competitors can see and report copied ads. Build original creative based on frameworks, not copies.
