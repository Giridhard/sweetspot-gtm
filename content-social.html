Photography & Video Style Guide for Food Content

Visual Identity:

  • Color palette: Warm golds, creams, sage greens as backgrounds. Natural wood, marble, or terracotta surfaces.
  • Lighting: Natural light strongly preferred. Side-lit or back-lit for depth. Avoid harsh overhead or flash.
  • Props: Brass utensils, copper cups, handmade ceramics, fresh herbs, whole spices, linen napkins. Modern-Indian aesthetic.
  • Hero product placement: [Brand Name] jar/sachet always visible in frame --- positioned at 1/3 mark, slightly out of focus or clearly in scene. Never hidden.
  • Hands in frame: Show hands stirring, pouring, sprinkling --- creates warmth and human connection.

Video Standards:

  • Resolution: 4K capture, export 1080p for social
  • Aspect ratios: 9:16 for Reels/Shorts, 16:9 for YouTube, 1:1 for feed posts
  • Speed: Recipe videos at 2-4x speed (timelapse), hero moments at real speed or 0.5x slow-mo
  • Text overlays: Inter font, gold (#D4A017) or white, drop shadow. Step numbers always visible.
  • Music: Upbeat, warm --- trending Indian audio when possible. Lo-fi for lifestyle shots.
  • Duration targets: Reels 15-30s, YouTube Shorts 30-59s, YouTube long-form 3-8 min

Talking Head Setup

Framing:

  • Medium close-up (chest to top of head)
  • Eyes at 1/3 from top of frame (rule of thirds)
  • Looking slightly off-camera or directly at lens
  • Headroom: small gap above head
  • Background: bookshelf, plants, kitchen, or branded backdrop

Lighting Setup:

  • Key light: 45 degrees from face, slightly above eye level
  • Fill light: Opposite side, lower intensity (or reflector)
  • For quick/casual: Ring light directly in front, camera in center
  • Avoid: overhead lighting, backlighting without fill, mixed color temperatures
