FSSAI License — Complete Walkthrough

1.1 Which License Type Does [Brand Name] Need?

FSSAI provides three tiers of licensing. The correct tier depends on annual turnover and operational scope.

License TypeEligibilityFee (Per Year)Validity
Basic RegistrationTurnover < ₹12 Lakh/year; petty food businesses, hawkers, tiny manufacturers₹1001–5 years
State LicenseTurnover ₹12 Lakh – ₹20 Crore/year; medium manufacturers, distributors, restaurants₹2,000 – ₹5,0001–5 years
Central LicenseTurnover > ₹20 Crore; OR importers/exporters; OR operating in multiple states₹7,5001–5 years (perpetual from Mar 2026)
[Brand Name] Recommendation: Start with a State License (turnover will be below ₹20 Cr in Year 1). However, if you plan to manufacture in one state and sell across India from Day 1 or import FOS raw material, you will need a Central License. If importing FOS: Central License is mandatory regardless of turnover.
2026 Update: From 10 March 2026, FSSAI license validity becomes perpetual — as long as you comply, no renewal is needed. This applies to new licenses issued after this date.

1.2 FSSAI Form B — Fields You Need to Fill

Form B is used for both State and Central License applications. It is filed online through the FoSCoS Portal (foscos.fssai.gov.in).

Part I — Business Entity Details

  • Type of business entity (Proprietorship / Partnership / Pvt Ltd / LLP)
  • Company name as per incorporation certificate
  • Registered office address with PIN code
  • PAN of the business entity
  • GST registration number (GSTIN)
  • Name, designation, and contact of the Authorized Signatory
  • Email address and mobile number

Part II — Premises Details

  • Address of food business premises (manufacturing/warehouse/office)
  • Whether owned or rented (proof required)
  • Area of premises in sq. ft.
  • GPS coordinates of premises

Part III — Food Category & Products

  • Food category selection (Sweetener / Dietary Supplement / Health Food)
  • List of all food products proposed to be manufactured
  • Expected production quantity per day/month
  • List of raw materials and their sources

Part IV — Technical Person Details

  • Name of technically qualified person in charge of operations
  • Qualification (Food Science / Food Technology / related degree)
  • Experience details
  • Photo ID proof of technical person

Part V — Food Safety Management Plan

  • HACCP plan or Food Safety Management System document
  • SOPs for manufacturing, storage, distribution
  • Recall plan
  • Pest control measures
  • Water source and treatment details

1.4 FoSCoS Portal — Step-by-Step Application Process

1 Create Account — Visit foscos.fssai.gov.in. Click "Sign Up." Enter your Aadhaar number, mobile, email. You will receive an OTP for verification. Create a username and password.
2 Select License Type — After login, click "Apply for New License/Registration." Select "State License" or "Central License" based on eligibility. For [Brand Name] importing FOS: select Central License.
3 Fill Form B Online — Complete all sections: Business entity details, premises details, food category (select "Health Supplements / Nutraceuticals / Functional Foods" + "Sweeteners"), product list, technical person information.
4 Upload Documents — Upload all documents from the checklist above. File format: PDF or JPEG. Max file size: 2 MB per document. Ensure all documents are legible and current.
5 Pay Fees Online — Central License: ₹7,500/year. You can apply for 1-5 years (pay ₹7,500 x number of years). Payment via net banking, debit card, credit card, or UPI.
6 Submit Application — Review all details. Submit. You receive an Application Reference Number (ARN). Download and save the acknowledgment.
7 Inspection — FSSAI authority schedules an inspection of your premises (for State/Central License). The inspector verifies: equipment, hygiene, storage, water supply, pest control, waste management.
8 License Issued — If satisfied, the Designated Officer (DO) grants the license within 60 days of application. License is available for download from the FoSCoS portal. Print and display at premises.
Deemed License: If FSSAI does not respond within 60 days, the license is deemed granted per Section 32 of the FSS Act. However, relying on this is risky — always follow up proactively.

1.6 Renewal Process

  • Apply for renewal at least 30 days before expiry
  • File through FoSCoS portal → "Renewal" option
  • Upload updated documents (any changes in premises, products, etc.)
  • Pay renewal fees (same as new license fees)
  • Late renewal: attracts penalty of ₹100 per day of delay
  • Post March 2026: Licenses become perpetual — no renewal needed (subject to annual compliance filings)

SECTION 02

Food Safety Standards — FOS Regulatory Classification

2.1 How FSSAI Classifies FOS (Fructooligosaccharides)

FOS occupies a unique regulatory position in India. It can be classified under multiple FSSAI frameworks depending on how it is positioned and marketed:

ClassificationApplicable RegulationRelevance to [Brand Name]
Prebiotic FoodFSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, etc.) Regulations, 2016 — Schedule IVPRIMARY FOS is a recognized prebiotic ingredient that modulates gut microbiota
Functional FoodFSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals, etc.) Regulations, 2016If marketed with health claims beyond sweetening
Sugar / Other SugarsFSS (Food Products Standards & Food Additives) Regulations, 2011 — Category 1702FOS as a bulk sweetener classified alongside other sugars
Food AdditiveFSS (Food Products Standards & Food Additives) Regulations, 2011If used as an additive/ingredient in other food products
Key Regulatory Finding: FOS is NOT a novel food in India. It has a history of human consumption and is already marketed by multiple brands (Nature's Velvet, Sharrets, etc.) with FSSAI certification. No novel food approval is needed. FOS is GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) globally.

2.2 FSS (Health Supplements, Nutraceuticals) Regulations, 2016

This is the primary regulation governing [Brand Name]'s positioning as a prebiotic sweetener.

Key Provisions:

  • Definition: "Prebiotic food" means food containing added non-viable food components that confer health benefits by modulation of gut microbiota
  • Maximum Prebiotic Limit: Not more than 40g per day per 2000 kcal for adults
  • Recommended Serving Limit for FOS: Up to 5g per serving in functional beverages; up to 15g/day total intake to minimize GI side effects
  • Product Approval: Products containing prebiotics need to comply with Schedule IV of the Regulations
  • Mandatory Claims Language: Must substantiate any health claim with scientific evidence
  • No Disease Cure Claims: Cannot claim to treat, cure, or prevent any disease
  • Label Requirement: Must state "PREBIOTIC FOOD" or "CONTAINS PREBIOTIC" on label
  • Compliance Date: All FBOs must comply from April 1, 2022 onwards

2.3 Novel Food Regulations — Does FOS Need Approval?

No Novel Food Approval Required. FOS has an established history of consumption worldwide and in India. Multiple FOS products are already FSSAI-licensed and sold in India. The FSS (Approval of Non-Specified Food and Food Ingredients) Regulations, 2017 applies only to ingredients without a history of human consumption. FOS does not fall in this category.

However, if [Brand Name] introduces a novel delivery format or novel combination (e.g., FOS + a new ingredient not in FSSAI's approved list), then the Non-Specified Food approval may be triggered:

  • Application fee: ₹50,000 + GST
  • Requires safety dossier with toxicological studies
  • FSSAI responds within 45 days for clarifications
  • Total timeline: 6-18 months
  • Avoid this by using only FSSAI-approved ingredients

3.7 Sample Collection Protocol

  • Collect samples from finished product lots using aseptic techniques
  • Minimum sample size: 500g (or as specified by the lab)
  • Pack in sterile, food-grade, airtight containers
  • Label with: batch number, date of manufacture, product name, quantity
  • Maintain cold chain (2–8°C) during transport for microbiological samples
  • Deliver to lab within 24 hours of collection
  • Retain duplicate samples for 30 days for re-testing if needed
  • Maintain all test reports for minimum 2 years
SECTION 04

Packaging & Labeling Compliance

4.1 Governing Regulations

Two primary regulations govern packaging and labeling for food products in India:

FSS (Labelling & Display) Regulations, 2020

Covers all food-specific labeling: product name, ingredients, nutritional info, allergens, FSSAI logo, date marking, veg/non-veg symbol, claims.

Legal Metrology (Packaged Commodities) Rules, 2011

Covers MRP declaration, net quantity, manufacturer details, country of origin, unit sale price. Enforced by Department of Consumer Affairs.

4.5 Health Claims — What Can and Cannot Be Said

Governed by FSS (Advertising & Claims) Regulations, 2018:

ClaimAllowed?Condition / Legal Definition
"Sugar Free"YESProduct must contain ≤0.5g sugar per 100g (solids) or 100ml (liquids). FOS at ≥95% purity qualifies.
"Low Calorie"YES (if true)Must contain ≤40 kcal per 100g (solids) or ≤20 kcal per 100ml (liquids). FOS at ~1.5-2 kcal/g may not qualify — verify with actual tested values.
"Prebiotic"YESPermitted if product meets Schedule IV of Nutraceuticals Regulations and FOS is the functional prebiotic ingredient. Must substantiate with evidence.
"Supports Gut Health"CONDITIONALPermitted only as a function claim backed by scientific evidence. Cannot be a disease claim. Wording matters: "Supports" is safer than "Improves."
"Suitable for Diabetics"NOThis constitutes a disease risk reduction claim which requires substantial scientific evidence and FSSAI pre-approval. Extremely risky to use without explicit approval.
"Cures/Treats Diabetes"NEVERAbsolute prohibition. No food product can claim to cure, treat, mitigate any disease under FSS Act.
"No Added Sugar"YESIf no sugar is added during processing. Must include: "CONTAINS NATURALLY OCCURRING SUGARS" if any natural sugars present.
"High in Fiber"YES (if true)Product must contain ≥6g dietary fiber per 100g (solids) or ≥3g per 100 kcal.
"Natural"CAUTIONFSSAI restricts this claim. Product must not contain any artificial or synthetic ingredients. FOS produced by enzymatic process may or may not qualify — verify manufacturing process.
"Zero Glycemic Index"CONDITIONALMust be backed by actual GI testing of the product. FOS has negligible GI but claim needs test evidence.
Penalty for False Claims: Under FSS Act, Section 53: misleading claims can result in fine up to ₹10 lakh. Section 52: false labeling can result in imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine up to ₹5 lakh. Always verify claims with a food regulatory consultant before printing.

6.2 GST Registration Requirements

  • Mandatory if annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for services / special category states)
  • Mandatory if selling through e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart) regardless of turnover
  • Register on gst.gov.in using PAN, Aadhaar, business address proof
  • Processing time: 7–15 working days
  • GSTIN is a 15-digit number based on state code + PAN

6.3 Input Tax Credit (ITC)

[Brand Name] can claim ITC on:

  • Raw material purchases (FOS powder, packaging materials) — 18% GST paid
  • Machinery and equipment for manufacturing
  • Professional services (legal, consulting, marketing)
  • Logistics and transportation
  • Rent (if GST-registered landlord)
  • Office supplies and utilities
Key ITC Rule: ITC can only be claimed if the supplier has filed their GSTR-1 return and the purchase appears in your GSTR-2B auto-populated statement. Ensure all suppliers are GST-compliant.

6.4 E-Way Bill Requirements

  • Generate on ewaybillgst.gov.in
  • Valid for distances up to 200 km: 1 day; additional 200 km: +1 day
  • Must be generated before goods movement begins
  • Both consignor and transporter need to carry e-way bill

SECTION 07

Import-Export Regulations

7.1 Import Export Code (IEC)

If [Brand Name] imports FOS raw material from international suppliers (e.g., from China, Japan, Belgium) or plans to export finished products, an IEC is mandatory.

1 Apply on DGFT Portal — Visit dgft.gov.in. Login with DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) or Aadhaar-based e-sign.
2 Fill Form ANF-2A — Business details, PAN, bank account, address proof. Entirely paperless.
3 Upload Documents — PAN card, Aadhaar, cancelled cheque/bank certificate, proof of business address, CIN (for companies).
4 Pay Fee — ₹500 (online)
5 IEC Issued — Typically within 3–5 working days. IEC is a 10-digit PAN-based code. Valid for lifetime (no renewal needed).

7.2 APEDA Registration (For Export)

Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority (APEDA) registration is required for exporting food products from India.

  • Eligibility: Must have valid IEC; business in export of agricultural/processed food
  • Apply online through DGFT centralized platform
  • Fee: ₹5,000 + GST
  • Documents: IEC certificate, bank certificate, address proof, digital signature
  • RCMC (Registration Cum Membership Certificate): Issued by APEDA, valid for 5 years
  • Benefits: Access to export promotion schemes, subsidies, quality certification support

7.3 Country-Specific Export Requirements

USA (FDA Requirements)

  • FDA Food Facility Registration (biennial renewal)
  • Prior Notice of imported food shipments
  • Foreign Supplier Verification Program (FSVP) compliance
  • FOS classified as Conventional Food or Dietary Supplement depending on use
  • GRAS status: FOS is GRAS — no pre-market approval needed
  • Nutrition Facts label per FDA 21 CFR 101
  • Supplement Facts panel if marketed as supplement
  • US Agent appointment required (for FDA communications)
  • Bioterrorism Act compliance

UAE (SFDA / ESMA Requirements)

  • Emirates Authority for Standardization and Metrology (ESMA) product registration
  • Halal certification (mandatory for food imports into UAE)
  • Arabic language labeling on all packages
  • UAE Conformity Assessment (ECAS) certificate
  • Health certificate from FSSAI / EIC
  • Certificate of Origin
  • Free Sale Certificate
  • Shelf life: minimum 50% remaining at port of entry

7.4 Import of FOS Raw Material

If importing FOS powder from international suppliers:

  • FSSAI import license (Central License covers this)
  • Customs clearance at port of entry
  • FSSAI port officer inspection and sampling
  • Lab testing of imported consignment (at importer's cost)
  • Certificate of Analysis (CoA) from supplier's lab
  • Import duty: depends on HSN code; typically 30–40% basic customs duty on sugars
  • IGST: 18% on assessable value
  • Customs broker appointment recommended for first-time importers

9.2 Product Liability — Deep Dive

This is the most critical insurance for a food startup. Under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, product liability claims can be filed against manufacturers, sellers, and service providers.

Coverage Elements:

  • Legal defense costs for product liability lawsuits
  • Compensation for bodily injury caused by the product
  • Product recall costs (if applicable)
  • Contamination liability (accidental contamination of food product)
  • Third-party property damage

Premium Factors:

  • Premium rates: typically 1% to 1.75% of the coverage limit
  • Recommended coverage: ₹50 Lakh to ₹2 Crore for first 2 years
  • Factors affecting premium: product type (food = higher risk), turnover, quality certifications, claims history
  • Having FSSAI compliance, HACCP, and ISO 22000 can reduce premiums by 10–20%

Recommended Insurers:

  • New India Assurance Co. Ltd. (government-owned, largest general insurer)
  • HDFC ERGO General Insurance
  • Tata AIG General Insurance
  • ICICI Lombard General Insurance
  • Bajaj Allianz General Insurance
