2.3 Quality Certificates Required

FSSAI License

Food Safety & Standards Authority of India manufacturing license. Category: Food Additives / Sweeteners. License No. to be printed on all B2B packaging.

ISO 22000:2018

Food Safety Management System certification. Essential for B2B credibility. Cost: Rs 2-3L for initial certification. Annual audit: Rs 80K-1L.

HACCP Certification

Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points. Often required by MNC food companies (HUL, Nestle, ITC). Cost: Rs 1.5-2L. Renewal: annual.

GMP Certificate

Good Manufacturing Practices. State FDA-issued. Required for all food manufacturing. Ensures facility hygiene, process control, traceability.

Certificate of Analysis (COA)

Batch-wise COA required by all B2B clients. Parameters: FOS purity (>95%), moisture (<5%), heavy metals, microbial count, shelf life.

Organic Certification (Optional)

India Organic / USDA Organic / EU Organic. If FOS source is organic chicory root. Premium: Rs 200-300/kg extra. Cost: Rs 3-5L for certification.

Halal / Kosher (Export)

Required for Middle East (Halal) and US/EU (Kosher) B2B sales. FOS is naturally compliant — just need certification. Cost: Rs 1-2L each.

Non-GMO Verification

Increasingly demanded by clean-label brands. Third-party verification. FOS from chicory root is inherently non-GMO.

3 Ingredient Branding — "Powered by [Brand Name] FOS"

In the food industry, NutraSweet and Splenda pioneered this approach with sweeteners, and [Brand Name] FOS can replicate this model for the Indian prebiotic market.

3.4 Food Industry Ingredient Branding Case Studies

Splenda (Sucralose) — Licensing Model

Splenda became the world's best-selling artificial sweetener by licensing its brand to food manufacturers. Key elements:

  • McNeil Nutritionals (J&J subsidiary) licensed Splenda brand to 4,500+ products globally
  • "Made with Splenda" logo generated consumer trust and product differentiation
