Brand Architecture & Name Strategy

Why "[Brand Name]" Cannot Be the Umbrella Brand

Critical Finding: The word "Sweet" creates a hard ceiling for portfolio expansion. It actively undermines beer positioning (consumers reject "sweet" beer), signals candy/sugar rather than health/prebiotics, and blocks future expansion into savory, protein, or functional categories. The AG1 rebrand (from Athletic Greens) proves that removing limiting words from a name unlocks growth — subscriptions went from under 100K to 750K+.

However, [Brand Name] should be KEPT as a sub-brand for the sweetener product only. It's an excellent sweetener name with an existing trademark. Rebrand risk is low because brand equity is still early-stage.

Brand Architecture: Hybrid (Endorsed) Model

Following the Tata Consumer playbook (Tata Tea, Tata Soulfull, Tata Salt), an endorsed hybrid model works best. A pure House of Brands (like ITC/Zydus) is too expensive for a startup. A pure Branded House (like Huel) doesn't work because the product categories are too diverse.

Contract Manufacturer Evaluation Checklist

  1. Certifications: FSSAI (mandatory), ISO 22000 (preferred), HACCP, GMP. Request copies.
  2. Facility audit: Visit in person. Check hygiene, pest control, water quality, equipment maintenance.
  3. Production capacity: Can they handle 2x your initial order? 5x in 12 months?
  4. Quality control: Do they have in-house lab? What testing do they do per batch?
  5. Reference check: Ask for 3 existing client references. Call them.
  6. Sample quality: Order 100-unit sample batch. Test for taste, consistency, shelf stability.
