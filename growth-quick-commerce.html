Step-by-Step Onboarding Process

  1. Vendor Portal Registration — Visit brands.blinkit.com. Register as a new vendor with GST number, PAN, FSSAI license, brand trademark certificate, company incorporation documents, and bank account details. No registration fee is charged.
  2. Category Approval — Select "Grocery & Staples > Sugar & Sweeteners" as your primary category. Submit product catalog with MRP, pack sizes, shelf life, ingredient list, nutritional information, and product images (white background, 2000x2000px minimum).
  3. Documentation Verification — Blinkit's team reviews: FSSAI central license (mandatory for multi-city), GST registration in each state of operation, product testing reports (lab certificates for food safety), trademark registration, and insurance documentation.
  4. Commercial Terms Negotiation — Negotiate commission rate (target 10-15% for health food category), inwarding fee (₹5/unit), fulfillment fee (₹50/unit). For a new brand, expect higher initial rates — can renegotiate after 6 months of volume.
  5. Listing Fee & Brand Credits — Pay approximately ₹25,000 per SKU per state as listing fee. This is returned fully as Brand Central ad credits, effectively making listing free if you plan to advertise (which you should).
  6. Sample Submission & Quality Check — Ship product samples to Blinkit's quality team. Products are checked for packaging integrity, barcode readability, weight accuracy, label compliance, and shelf life verification.
  7. Warehouse Inwarding — Ship initial inventory to Blinkit's central/regional warehouses. They distribute to dark stores based on demand algorithms. Start with top 5 cities: Delhi NCR, Bangalore, Mumbai, Pune, Hyderabad. Ship 15-20 days of buffer stock.
  8. Listing Goes Live — Product appears in search results and category pages. First 30 days get "New" badge — critical window for visibility. Immediately activate advertising to capitalize on new product boost.

Dark Store Model & Distribution

How Dark Stores Work

  • Centralized inwarding: You ship to regional hubs (not individual stores)
  • Algorithmic distribution: Blinkit's system auto-distributes stock across dark stores based on demand density, historical orders, and category velocity
  • Store size: Typical dark store is 1,500-3,000 sq ft, holding 5,000-8,000 SKUs
  • Stock level: Each dark store holds 5-15 units per SKU; auto-replenished from hub
  • Franchise model: Many stores are franchise-operated; Blinkit manages inventory centrally

Packaging Requirements for Dark Stores

Mandatory Packaging Specs

  • Barcode: EAN-13 standard, clearly printed, scannable from 30cm distance. Must be on flat surface (not curved). Test with barcode scanner before shipping.
  • Weight tolerance: ±2% of declared weight. QC teams weigh-check random samples.
  • Dimensions: Must fit standard dark store shelving (max 30cm height, 20cm width, 15cm depth for pouches). Jar packaging gets bottom-shelf placement.
  • Inner packaging: Individual units must be separately scannable. No loose packing — each unit sealed.
  • Outer carton: Master carton of 12-24 units. Clearly labeled with SKU, quantity, batch, expiry. Platform-specific carton labels required.

FSSAI Label Compliance

  • FSSAI license number prominently on front of pack
  • Nutritional Information Panel: Energy, protein, carbohydrate, sugar, fat, fiber per serving and per 100g
  • Ingredient list: Full ingredient declaration in descending order of weight
  • Allergen declaration: "Contains" statement for any applicable allergens
  • Best before date: Minimum 6 months remaining at time of inwarding. FOS advantage: 18-24 month shelf life — platforms prefer long shelf-life products.
  • Veg/Non-veg symbol: Green dot (vegetarian) mandatory
  • MRP declaration: "MRP inclusive of all taxes: ₹XXX" in readable font

Inventory Management — The Critical Factor

WARNING: Out-of-Stock = Ranking Death

Going out of stock on a QC platform has consequences far worse than just lost sales:

  • Algorithm penalty: Even 1-2 days OOS drops your ranking significantly. Recovery takes 2-4 weeks of consistent sales.
  • Customer loss: Customer who searches "sweetener" and doesn't find you will buy Sugar Free instead. You may never get them back.
  • Ad waste: If you're running ads and go OOS, you've wasted your entire ad spend building momentum that's now lost.
  • "Ghost listing" risk: Extended OOS (7+ days) may cause platform to delist your product. Re-listing requires going through onboarding again.

What Works vs What Doesn't

Distilled from 100+ brand experiences on Indian QC platforms. These are the patterns that separate brands that scale from brands that burn cash and fail.

WHAT WORKS

Small pack sizes (₹99-199 impulse range)
QC customers buy on impulse. A ₹99 trial pack has 3x the conversion rate of a ₹399 pack. Get them to try → build habit → upsell to larger packs.
Category bundling (sweetener + tea = combo deal)
Bundle [Brand Name] with complementary products: green tea, coffee, oats, almond milk. Increases basket value and introduces your product to adjacent-category buyers.
Weekend promotions (health-conscious weekend shoppers)
Saturday-Sunday sees 30-40% higher health food sales on QC. Run weekend-only offers: "Weekend Wellness — 15% off [Brand Name]." Health-conscious shoppers over-index on weekends.
New product badge exploitation (first 30 days)
Platforms boost new products for 30 days with a "New" badge and algorithmic preference. This is your golden window. Combine with heavy ad spend + coupon to maximize trial during this period.
Repeat purchase — sweetener is consumable
Unlike one-time purchases (electronics, household items), sweetener is consumed and reordered every 30-45 days. QC platforms reward repeat-purchase products with higher rankings because they drive platform stickiness.
Festival season offers (Diwali sweet-making)
October-November is peak sweetener season (Diwali mithai). "Make guilt-free Diwali sweets with [Brand Name]" — a ready-made campaign. Stock 2x and run banner ads during this period.
Review generation (inserts + follow-ups)
Include a card inside packaging: "Love [Brand Name]? Rate us on [platform] — it helps other health-conscious customers find us." 4.2+ star rating is the threshold for algorithmic boost.
Dual category listing (sweetener + gut health)
Request placement in both "Sugar Substitutes" AND "Health & Wellness / Gut Health" categories. FOS prebiotic positioning enables this dual listing. 40-60% more visibility.
Consistent stock levels (never, ever run out)
The #1 rule of QC success. Maintain 15-20 day buffer. Set up SMS alerts for low-stock triggers. One OOS event can undo months of ranking work.

WHAT DOESN'T WORK

Large/premium packs (₹500+ fails on QC)
QC customers want small, quick, affordable. ₹500+ products have 60-70% lower conversion on QC vs marketplace. Save premium packs for Amazon/D2C. On QC, ₹199 is the sweet spot.
No advertising spend (invisible among 10,000 SKUs)
Without ads, your product is buried on page 5+ of search results. Organic discovery on QC is nearly impossible for new brands. Budget ₹30-50K/month per platform — non-negotiable.
Inconsistent stock (OOS kills ranking permanently)
Every OOS event resets your ranking momentum. Brands that go OOS 2-3 times in 6 months see 50%+ drop in organic visibility. Some never recover.
Ignoring platform-specific search terms
Each platform has different search patterns. "Sugar free" on Blinkit is a brand search (Sugar Free Natura). "Sweetener" is a category search. Target category terms, not brand terms of competitors.
Competing on price alone (QC = convenience buyers)
QC customers pay for convenience — they're already paying ₹25-40 platform fees. They don't price-compare obsessively. Compete on VALUE (prebiotic + sweetener) not just price.
Expecting instant volume (takes 3-6 months)
QC is not a "list and sell" channel. It takes 3-6 months to build velocity, rankings, reviews, and repeat-purchase base. Budget for 6 months of investment before expecting ROI.
Same listing across all platforms
Each platform has different image specs, description lengths, keyword patterns, and customer demographics. Optimize each listing separately. What works on Blinkit may not work on Zepto.
Pausing ads to "save money"
Pausing ads causes velocity drop → ranking drops → organic sales drop → total revenue drops more than ad cost saved. Always-on advertising is the cost of doing business on QC.
Ignoring reviews and ratings
A 3.8-star product gets 50% fewer clicks than a 4.3-star product on QC. Actively manage reviews: respond to negatives, fix product issues, use review generation inserts.

The real value: every QC trial customer who likes [Brand Name] becomes a potential D2C subscriber or Amazon repeat buyer at 2-3x the margin. QC is the top of your funnel.
