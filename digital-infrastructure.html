1.4 FAQ Page — 30 Questions (/pages/faq)

Q1: What is FOS (Fructooligosaccharides)?
FOS is a naturally occurring prebiotic fiber found in chicory root, bananas, onions, and garlic. It tastes sweet but is not digested like sugar — instead, it feeds beneficial gut bacteria in your large intestine, supporting digestive health and immunity.
Q2: How sweet is [Brand Name] compared to sugar?
[Brand Name] FOS is approximately 30-50% as sweet as table sugar. For most beverages, use 1.5-2 teaspoons of [Brand Name] where you would use 1 teaspoon of sugar. Over time, your taste buds adjust and you may find you need less.
Q3: Is [Brand Name] safe for diabetics?
Yes. FOS has a glycemic index of zero, meaning it does not raise blood sugar levels. However, we always recommend consulting your doctor before making dietary changes, especially if you are on medication.
Q4: Does it have any calories?
FOS contains approximately 1.5-2 calories per gram, compared to 4 calories per gram for sugar. A typical serving (5g) has only about 8-10 calories, and most of this energy goes to feeding gut bacteria rather than being absorbed by your body.
Q5: Can I use [Brand Name] for baking?
Yes, but with adjustments. FOS is heat-stable up to 140°C. For baking, replace sugar at a 1:0.7 ratio (use 70g of [Brand Name] for every 100g of sugar). Note that FOS does not caramelize like sugar, so browning will be different. It works best in cakes, cookies, and muffins.
Q6: What does it taste like? Is there an aftertaste?
[Brand Name] has a clean, mildly sweet taste similar to sugar but lighter. Unlike stevia, there is no bitter or metallic aftertaste. Most customers describe it as the closest natural sweetener to sugar in terms of taste profile.
Q7: How much FOS should I consume daily?
Research suggests 5-15 grams of FOS per day for prebiotic benefits. Start with 5g (1 teaspoon) per day and gradually increase to avoid initial digestive adjustment. Most people comfortably consume 10-15g daily within a week.
Q8: Are there any side effects?
Some people may experience mild bloating or gas when first starting FOS, as your gut microbiome adjusts. This is normal and typically subsides within 3-5 days. Start with small amounts and increase gradually. If symptoms persist beyond a week, reduce your intake.
Q9: Is [Brand Name] safe for children?
FOS is naturally present in many foods children already eat (bananas, onions). [Brand Name] is safe for children above 2 years in moderate amounts (3-5g/day). For infants and toddlers under 2, please consult your pediatrician.
Q10: Is it safe during pregnancy?
FOS occurs naturally in many foods and is generally considered safe during pregnancy. However, as with any dietary supplement, we recommend consulting your gynecologist before adding it to your routine during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
Q11: Where is the FOS sourced from?
Our FOS is extracted from chicory root (Cichorium intybus). We source pharmaceutical-grade FOS with a purity of 95%+ to ensure maximum prebiotic benefit and clean taste.
Q12: Is [Brand Name] FSSAI approved?
Yes. [Brand Name] is manufactured in an FSSAI-licensed facility and our product carries a valid FSSAI license number displayed on every package. FOS is an approved food ingredient under FSSAI regulations.
Q13: How is [Brand Name] different from stevia?
Unlike stevia, [Brand Name] FOS is a prebiotic that actively benefits gut health. Stevia is calorie-free but has a noticeable aftertaste and no prebiotic properties. FOS has minimal calories, no aftertaste, and feeds beneficial gut bacteria. It is a fundamentally different molecule with additional health benefits beyond sweetness.
Q14: How is it different from sugar alcohols (xylitol, erythritol)?
Sugar alcohols can cause significant digestive distress (laxative effect) and have no prebiotic benefit. FOS is a fiber, not a sugar alcohol. It feeds good bacteria rather than drawing water into the intestine, so the digestive experience is much gentler.
Q15: Can I add it to hot beverages?
Absolutely. [Brand Name] dissolves well in hot tea, coffee, and milk. It is heat-stable and maintains its sweetness and prebiotic properties in hot beverages up to 140°C.
Q16: What is the shelf life?
[Brand Name] has a shelf life of 24 months from the date of manufacture when stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Once opened, use within 6 months and keep the lid tightly sealed.
Q17: Does it dissolve in cold water?
Yes. FOS is highly soluble in both hot and cold water. It dissolves easily in cold beverages, smoothies, and yogurt without clumping.
Q18: Is [Brand Name] keto-friendly?
FOS is a fiber and most of it is not absorbed as glucose. It has minimal impact on blood sugar and ketone levels. However, strict keto followers should note that FOS does contain some caloric value from fermentation. It is suitable for moderate low-carb and keto-adjacent diets.
Q19: What are the prebiotic benefits?
FOS feeds Bifidobacteria and Lactobacilli in your gut. Regular consumption (5-15g/day) has been shown to improve bowel regularity, enhance calcium absorption, support immune function, reduce pathogenic bacteria, and may help reduce cholesterol levels.
Q20: Do you offer a subscription?
Yes. Subscribe & Save gives you 10% off every order, free shipping, and flexible delivery every 30, 45, or 60 days. You can pause, skip, or cancel anytime from your account dashboard. No commitment required.
Q21: What is your return policy?
We offer a 30-day satisfaction guarantee. If you are not happy with [Brand Name], contact us within 30 days of delivery for a full refund. The product must be at least 50% unused. We cover return shipping costs.
Q22: How long does shipping take?
Metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Kolkata): 2-3 business days. Tier 2 cities: 3-5 business days. Rest of India: 5-7 business days. Free shipping on all orders above ₹499.
Q23: Do you offer Cash on Delivery?
Yes. COD is available for orders up to ₹2,000 across India. A small COD handling fee of ₹40 applies. Prepaid orders enjoy free shipping above ₹499 and faster processing.
Q24: Is the packaging eco-friendly?
We are committed to sustainability. Our jars are made from food-grade recyclable PET. Shipping boxes are made from recycled corrugated cardboard. We use no single-use plastic in our packaging. We are working toward compostable pouches for future product lines.
Q25: Can I use [Brand Name] if I have IBS?
FOS is a FODMAP and may trigger symptoms in some IBS patients, particularly those sensitive to fructans. If you have IBS, start with a very small amount (2g) and monitor your response. Consult your gastroenterologist before regular use.
Q26: Is it vegan and gluten-free?
Yes. [Brand Name] is 100% plant-based (from chicory root), vegan, gluten-free, soy-free, dairy-free, and contains no major allergens. It is suitable for virtually all dietary preferences.
Q27: Can I use it in my restaurant or cafe?
Absolutely. We offer bulk/wholesale pricing for food service businesses. Contact us at wholesale@[brandname].in or fill out our B2B inquiry form for custom pricing on orders of 5kg and above.
Q28: Do you have any clinical studies supporting FOS benefits?
Yes. There are over 200 published peer-reviewed studies on FOS prebiotic benefits. Key studies include research from the Journal of Nutrition (2015) on bifidogenic effects, and the British Journal of Nutrition (2018) on calcium absorption. We link to these studies on our Science page.
Q29: Is this the same as inulin?
FOS and inulin are closely related — both are fructans from chicory root. FOS has shorter chain lengths (2-4 units) compared to inulin (2-60 units). This means FOS is sweeter, more soluble, and fermented faster in the gut. [Brand Name] uses short-chain FOS for optimal taste and rapid prebiotic action.
Q30: How do I track my order?
Once your order ships, you will receive a tracking link via email and WhatsApp. You can also track your order anytime by visiting [brandname].in/track or clicking "Track Order" in your account dashboard. We use Shiprocket for logistics, which provides real-time tracking.

6.6 Broadcast Rules & Best Practices

  • Frequency: Maximum 2 marketing broadcasts per week. Over-messaging leads to blocks and template quality degradation.
  • Best Send Times: Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM – 12 PM or 7 PM – 9 PM IST
  • Segmentation: Never send the same broadcast to everyone. Segment by: purchase history, product interest, city, engagement level
  • Opt-Out Handling: Include "Reply STOP to unsubscribe" in every marketing template. Process opt-outs within 24 hours. Maintain a suppression list.
  • Template Quality: Meta rates templates by quality (Green/Yellow/Red). If quality drops to Red, the template is paused. Keep templates personalized and relevant.
  • Content Mix: 60% value content (recipes, tips, education), 30% promotional (offers, launches), 10% engagement (polls, quizzes)
  • Compliance: Only message contacts who have explicitly opted in. Collect opt-in via Shopify checkout, signup forms, or WhatsApp widget initial consent.
  • Measurement: Track: delivery rate (>95%), read rate (>70%), click rate (>5%), opt-out rate (<1%), reply rate (>3%)
