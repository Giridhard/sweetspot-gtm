1.3 — Sub-Brand Naming Convention

All sub-brands follow the pattern: [Brand Name] [Descriptor]. The descriptor must be a single word that immediately communicates the product format or audience.

Current

[Brand Name] (Original)

Powder format, 1g sachets and jar. The hero SKU. FOS prebiotic sweetener for daily use in beverages and cooking.

Powder 1g sachet 100g jar
Phase 2

[Brand Name] Drops

Liquid FOS concentrate in a 30ml dropper bottle. For beverages where powder dissolution is impractical (cold drinks, smoothies).

Liquid 30ml dropper
Phase 2

[Brand Name] Bake

Heat-stable FOS blend optimized for baking. Measures cup-for-cup like sugar. Targeted at home bakers and bakery partners.

Powder 250g pouch 1kg bag
Phase 3

[Brand Name] Kids

Child-friendly format with fun flavors (mango, strawberry). Lower sweetness intensity. Pediatrician-endorsed positioning.

Flavored Stick packs
Naming Rule: Never use more than one descriptor word. "[Brand Name] Sugar Free Baking Mix" is incorrect. "[Brand Name] Bake" is correct. The descriptor must work as both a noun and an adjective.

1.4 — Logo Lockup Rules

Lockup A — [Brand Name] Standalone (Primary)

Used in 90% of consumer-facing contexts. The [Brand Name] wordmark appears alone without any GutCraft reference. This is the default lockup.

Lockup B — [Brand Name] + GutCraft Endorsement

[Brand Name] wordmark with "A GutCraft Product" tagline below, separated by a 0.5x hairline rule. Used on packaging, corporate communications, and trade materials.

Lockup C — Sub-Brand Format

"[Brand Name]" in primary weight + descriptor in lighter weight on the same baseline. Example: [Brand Name] Drops

Lockup D — Corporate / Investor

GutCraft logo at primary size, with "[Brand Name]" as a product callout. Used only in investor decks, board materials, and B2B partnership documents.

Critical Rule: Never combine Lockup C and Lockup B — sub-brands do not show the GutCraft endorsement on their primary logo. It appears only on packaging back panels.

Typography System.

Our type system balances heritage and modernity — a serif for brand presence and a sans-serif for clarity. Typography is the most critical element of brand recognition after color.

4.1 — Primary Typeface: Playfair Display

Why Playfair Display?

Playfair Display is a transitional serif with high contrast between thick and thin strokes. Its design heritage references the Enlightenment era — evoking trust, knowledge, and craftsmanship. In the Indian sweetener market dominated by clinical sans-serif brands (Sugar Free uses Helvetica-style type), Playfair provides differentiation that feels premium, trustworthy, and warm rather than pharmaceutical.

Why Inter?

Inter was specifically designed for computer screens. Its tall x-height ensures legibility at small sizes — critical for nutritional information, legal disclaimers, and mobile interfaces. With tabular figures and extensive language support (including Devanagari via companion fonts), Inter covers all our digital and print needs. It is the most readable sans-serif at sizes below 14px.

5.3 — Illustration Style Guidelines

Do Use
  • Warm, organic line art with gold accents on dark
  • Flowing lines that suggest digestive wellness and nature
  • Botanical elements: leaves, flowers, honey drips, fruits
  • Abstract gut-flora patterns (friendly circles and curves)
  • Hand-drawn textured elements for social media
  • Diverse Indian characters in warm, aspirational settings
  • Soft gradients in gold-to-amber range

Do Not Use
  • Clinical / pharmaceutical illustration styles
  • Anatomical diagrams of intestines or organs
  • Microscopic / scientific imaging of bacteria
  • Stock art with Western models or settings
  • 3D renders or photorealistic CGI
  • Neon colors, cyberpunk, or tech-forward aesthetics
  • Cartoonish / childish illustrations (except [Brand Name] Kids)
  • Complex infographic styles with dense data visualization
Section 06

Photography Style Guide.

Photography is the emotional anchor of [Brand Name]'s visual identity. Every image should feel warm, natural, and authentically Indian — evoking the feeling of a good morning, not a clinical setting.

6.1 — Product Photography

Lighting

Warm, directional key light at 45-degree angle from upper left. Golden hour feel with color temperature around 3500K-4500K. Soft fill light from opposite side. Avoid harsh specular highlights on packaging.

Key Light45° upper left, warm diffused
Fill LightBounce card, right side, 1:3 ratio
Color Temp3500K – 4500K
ModifierSoftbox or scrim, never bare flash
Camera Settings

Use a 50mm equivalent lens for natural perspective. Shallow depth of field to draw attention to the product with gentle background bokeh.

Lens50mm equivalent (full frame)
Aperturef/2.8 – f/4
ISO100-400 (low noise)
FormatRAW + JPEG, Adobe RGB color space

12.4 — Quality Control Checklist for Printed Materials

Pre-Print Approval Checklist
  • Logo is correct version (check against master SVG file)
  • Clear space around logo is maintained (1x cap-height)
  • Colors match Pantone references (request press proof for spot colors)
  • CMYK values are correct (verify against this manual)
  • Fonts are embedded or outlined (no system font substitution)
  • Images are 300 DPI minimum at final print size
  • Bleed extends 3mm beyond trim on all sides
  • Safe zone: all text 5mm inside trim edge
  • FSSAI license number is present and correct on all packaging
  • Hindi/bilingual text has been proofread by native speaker
  • Barcode/QR code scans correctly (test with phone camera)
  • MRP, net weight, and date markings are legible and compliant
  • Vegetarian mark (green square with circle) is correctly placed
  • Paper stock and finish match specification
  • Die-cut alignment is verified on physical mock-up
  • Color proof (soft proof or Cromalin) has been signed off

A.4 — Glossary of Brand Terms

TermDefinition
FOSFructooligosaccharides — naturally occurring prebiotic fibers found in fruits, vegetables, and honey. The core ingredient in [Brand Name].
PrebioticA type of dietary fiber that feeds beneficial bacteria in the gut. Distinct from probiotics (which are live bacteria).
GI IndexGlycemic Index — a measure of how quickly food raises blood sugar. [Brand Name]'s GI is 0 (does not raise blood sugar).
Endorsed BrandBrand architecture where the sub-brand leads consumer communication with the parent brand as a quiet endorser. [Brand Name]'s relationship to GutCraft.
LockupA specific, approved arrangement of logo elements (wordmark, tagline, endorser) that must be used as a unit. Never rearranged.
Clear SpaceThe minimum empty area surrounding the logo where no other visual elements may intrude. Measured as 1x the cap-height of the "S" character.
Spot ColorA specifically mixed ink (e.g., Pantone 7405 C) used in printing for exact color reproduction, as opposed to process CMYK simulation.
Safe ZoneThe minimum distance from the edge of a printed piece where text or critical elements must not be placed, to account for cutting variation.
BleedThe area extending beyond the final trim size where background colors or images extend, ensuring no white edges after cutting (typically 3mm).
Soft-Touch LaminationA matte finish applied to printed materials that creates a velvety, premium tactile experience. Used on [Brand Name] business cards and packaging.
