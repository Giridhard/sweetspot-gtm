WHAT CUSTOMERS LOVE (Across All Brands)

  • Good for diabetic diets
  • Wide range of sweetener types (stevia, xylitol, erythritol)

Monk Fruit Products (Zeeero, SweeTempt, Beyond The Sugar)

  • "No bitter aftertaste" — most cited positive
  • "Clean sweetness very close to real sugar"
  • No digestive issues reported with allulose blends
  • "No erythritol" positioning resonates strongly

[Brand Name]

  • "No aftertaste in tea and coffee"
  • "Ideal for substituting refined sugar at home"
  • Gut health benefits appreciated
  • Tastes close to actual sugar

WHAT CUSTOMERS HATE (Across All Brands)

Sugar Free (Gold/Green/Natura)

  • Aspartame safety concerns — "chemicals," "artificial"
  • Aftertaste complaints for Gold variant
  • Stevia Green variant: "not sweet enough" for some
  • Growing consumer shift away from artificial sweeteners

Stevia Products (General)

  • Bitter aftertaste — #1 complaint across stevia brands
  • "Requires adjustment period" for taste
  • "Not sweet enough" — dosing confusion
  • Some brands taste "medicinal"

Erythritol Products

  • "No sweetness at all" — some users report zero sweetness
  • Cleveland Clinic 2023 study raised cardiovascular concerns
  • "Cooling effect" in mouth disliked by some
  • Mixed quality and value for money reviews

Xylitol Products

  • "Damn expensive" — price is #1 complaint
  • "Very low sweetness" for some users
  • Digestive discomfort at higher doses

INJIYA Stevia

  • "Worst product. Does not sweeten anything"
  • "Have to put a lot of it to get sweetness but makes it bitter"
  • Pure leaf powder format polarizes users

General Consumer Concerns

  • Growing distrust of "chemical-sounding" ingredients
  • Gut microbiome disruption fears (saccharin, sucralose)
  • WHO classification of aspartame as "possibly carcinogenic"
  • Consumers want "simplicity" — fewer ingredients preferred
