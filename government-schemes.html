1. Startup India — DPIIT Recognition

The foundational certification that unlocks dozens of other benefits. Apply first, everything else follows.

APPLY FIRST What is DPIIT Startup Recognition?

DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) recognition is the Government of India's official certification that your entity qualifies as a "startup." It is completely free and typically approved within 7–14 days. This single registration unlocks access to tax holidays, IPR rebates, self-certification under labor laws, Seed Fund eligibility, government procurement (GeM) access, and more.

2026 Update: The 2026 framework (G.S.R. 108(E), dated 4 Feb 2026) introduces a Deep Tech category with a 20-year benefit window, expanded entity types, and increased turnover caps of Rs 200 Cr / Rs 300 Cr. The incorporation deadline has been extended to 1 April 2030 via Union Budget 2025–26.

Key Benefits Unlocked

3-Year Tax Holiday (Section 80-IAC)

100% deduction on profits for any 3 consecutive years within the first 10 years of incorporation. Choose the 3 most profitable years strategically.

Self-Certification under 9 Laws

Self-certify compliance under 6 labor laws and 3 environmental laws instead of going through inspections. Valid for 3–5 years after incorporation.

Fast-Track Patent Filing (80% Rebate)

Pay only Rs 1,600 for patent filing (vs Rs 8,000 for companies). Expedited examination at Rs 8,000 (vs Rs 60,000). Government bears facilitator fees.

Fund of Funds Access (SIDBI)

Access to Rs 10,000 Cr Fund of Funds managed by SIDBI. Funds are routed through SEBI-registered AIFs that invest in startups.

Seed Fund Eligibility (SISFS)

Up to Rs 20 lakh as milestone-based grant for proof of concept. Up to Rs 50 lakh as convertible debt for commercialization.

GeM Procurement Access

Register on Government e-Marketplace for government procurement. 20% of government purchases reserved for MSMEs/startups.

Application Process — Step by Step

Register on Startup India Portal

Visit startupindia.gov.in. Create an account using your company email. Complete the entity profile with CIN number, date of incorporation, and business description.

Submit Recognition Application via NSWS Portal

Applications now route through the National Single Window System (NSWS). Upload Certificate of Incorporation, PAN of the entity, and a brief write-up explaining your innovation (FOS-based sugar substitute using biotechnology).

Provide Innovation Description

Upload Supporting Documents

Letter of recommendation from an incubator (T-Hub or WE-Hub, if available), OR a patent filing acknowledgment, OR a letter from a recognized funding body. At least one of these strengthens the application.

Await Approval (7–14 Days)

DPIIT reviews the application. If approved, you receive a Recognition Certificate and a unique Startup India Recognition Number. This number is used for all subsequent scheme applications.

Apply for 80-IAC Tax Exemption Separately

After DPIIT recognition, apply separately for the Section 80-IAC tax holiday through the Startup India portal. This requires an Inter-Ministerial Board (IMB) evaluation. Approval rate is historically low (~1.8%), so prepare a strong innovation dossier.

To improve your chances: (1) File at least one patent before applying, (2) Show clear product-market innovation beyond just selling a commodity, (3) Provide evidence of R&D expenditure. As of February 2026, DPIIT has cleared 187 startups under the revised framework in a single batch, suggesting faster processing.

2. PMFME — PM Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises

35% capital subsidy for food processing units. Directly relevant for [Brand Name] manufacturing.

SUBSIDY Scheme Overview

Launched by the Ministry of Food Processing Industries (MoFPI) under the Atmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyan, PMFME is one of the most directly applicable schemes for [Brand Name]'s manufacturing operations. It provides credit-linked capital subsidies to micro food processing enterprises for upgradation, modernization, and new unit setup.

35% Capital Subsidy

Credit-linked subsidy at 35% of eligible project cost, with a maximum ceiling of Rs 10 lakh per unit for individual/group category micro enterprises.

ODOP Priority

Products under "One District One Product" (ODOP) receive priority processing. Check if your district has a relevant ODOP category.

Common Infrastructure

Access to common infrastructure including food testing labs, cold storage, and packaging units set up under the scheme.

Training & Capacity Building

Free training programs for food processing unit owners on compliance, quality, packaging, and marketing.

Application Process

Online Registration on PMFME Portal

Visit pmfme.mofpi.gov.in. Complete registration to receive User ID and Password. Login and access the "Apply Online" option on the dashboard.

Submit Project Details

Provide business details, location (Hyderabad, Telangana), investment information, and product details (FOS-based sweetener). Clearly describe the food processing activity — manufacturing of fructooligosaccharide-based sugar substitute.

Upload Documents

Aadhaar card, PAN card, bank details, two passport-size photographs, FSSAI license/registration, address proof, business registration certificate.

District Resource Person (DRP) Verification

A DRP assigned to your district will verify the application, inspect the premises if needed, and validate the project details.

Bank Loan Sanction

Since the subsidy is credit-linked, your bank processes the loan application. The 35% subsidy is credited to your "mirror account" once the loan is approved and the project is implemented.

Subsidy Disbursement

The subsidy is typically released after the bank confirms that the loan has been disbursed and the food processing unit is operational or under setup.

5. DSIR — Department of Scientific & Industrial Research

Get your R&D center officially recognized. Unlocks customs duty exemptions, tax deductions, and credibility for grant applications.

TAX BENEFIT Recognition as In-House R&D Center

DSIR recognition validates your company's R&D capabilities and unlocks significant fiscal incentives. For a biotech company like Revelations Biotech, this is a powerful credential.

Benefits of DSIR Recognition

Customs Duty Exemption

Exemption from Basic Customs Duty on import of specified equipment, instruments, spares, and consumables for R&D. For biotech companies, this extends to both R&D AND production activities.

R&D Tax Deduction

Eligible for up to 150% of IT returns on capital and revenue expenses incurred on R&D-based projects. No lower or upper cap on R&D expenditure.

Concessional IGST

Maximum applicable IGST capped at 5% for purchases made for the recognized R&D unit (vs standard 12–18%).

Gateway to Government Grants

DSIR recognition is frequently a mandatory or preferred eligibility criterion for grants from DST, DBT, BIRAC, CSIR, and TDB.

Market Credibility

Powerful third-party validation of R&D capabilities. Strengthens investor pitch, B2B credibility, and partnership proposals.

Biotech Startup Exception

For biotech startups, the standard requirement of 3 years of operational history is WAIVED. You can apply immediately.

TAX Angel Tax — Section 56(2)(viib) — ABOLISHED

Major Relief for 2026: Angel Tax has been completely abolished effective 1 April 2025 through the Finance Act, 2024. This means:
  • No angel tax on new fundraises from 1 April 2025 onwards — issue shares at any premium without tax liability
  • No DPIIT exemption application needed specifically for angel tax
  • No merchant banker valuation requirement for angel tax purposes
  • Foreign investors can invest at any premium without angel tax consequences
  • Legacy cases (pre-April 2025 fundraises) may still face assessment if notices are issued
What This Means for Revelations Biotech: You can raise funds from angel investors, family offices, or VCs at any valuation premium without worrying about the company being taxed on the premium received. This was previously a major pain point where startups had to justify their valuation or face tax on the "excess" premium. The abolition removes this friction entirely for all future fundraises.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not applying early enough: Most schemes have eligibility windows tied to incorporation date. SISFS requires incorporation within 2 years, BIRAC BIG within 5 years. Start applying from Day 1 of incorporation.
2. Not stacking benefits: Central and State schemes are designed to be complementary. You can claim PMFME subsidy (Central) + TS Investment Subsidy (State) + SGST Reimbursement (State) + CGTMSE Guarantee (Central) on the same project. Most founders apply for one scheme and stop.
3. Missing BIRAC call deadlines: BIG calls open only twice a year (Jan 1 and Jul 1) for ~45 days. Missing the window means waiting 6 months. Set calendar reminders for December 15 and June 15 to start preparing.
4. Not getting DSIR recognition before importing: If you import equipment without DSIR recognition, you pay full customs duty. Get recognized first, then import. The savings can be 15–25% on imports.
5. Choosing 80-IAC years too early: Once you elect the 3 consecutive years, you cannot change them. If you choose years when profits are low, the tax saving is minimal. Wait for peak profitability years.
6. Forgetting to claim SGST reimbursement: Telangana offers 100% SGST refund for 5 years, but you must file claims proactively. This is not automatic. Many companies forget and lose out on lakhs annually.
7. Not maintaining R&D records for DSIR: DSIR certification requires annual R&D expenditure reporting via Form 3CL. Maintain meticulous records of all R&D spending — salaries, consumables, equipment, outsourced testing — from Day 1.
