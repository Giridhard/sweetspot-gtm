Section 3

Amazon India Advertising — Step-by-Step Execution

Sponsored Products — Complete Setup

1
Prerequisites
Active Amazon Seller Central account with Brand Registry. Products listed with optimized titles (include primary keyword in first 80 chars), 7+ images (main image on white background, lifestyle shots, infographics, ingredient close-ups), bullet points with keywords, and FSSAI license number displayed on listing.
2
Create Auto Campaign (Discovery Phase)
Go to: Campaign Manager > Create Campaign > Sponsored Products > Automatic Targeting.
Name: [ProductName]_SP_Auto_[Date]
Daily budget: Rs 500-1,000
Default bid: Rs 15
Targeting groups: Enable all four — Close Match, Loose Match, Substitutes, Complements
Bid adjustments: Close Match +25%, Loose Match 0%, Substitutes +10%, Complements -20%
Run duration: 14-21 days minimum before harvesting keywords
3
Harvest Keywords from Auto Campaign
After 14 days: Download Search Term Report (Advertising > Reports > Search Term). Filter for terms with 3+ conversions — these are your proven keywords. Also note terms with high clicks (10+) but zero conversions — add these as negative keywords in the auto campaign.
4
Create Manual Campaigns
Campaign A — Exact Match: Add proven keywords (3+ conversions) as exact match. Set bids at your target ACOS. Start at Rs 12-20 per keyword.
Campaign B — Broad Match: Add same keywords as broad match at 70% of exact match bid. This discovers new long-tail variations.
Campaign C — Product Targeting: Target competitor ASINs (Sugar Free, Stevia products, etc.) — bid Rs 10-15.
5
Negative Keyword Management
In auto campaign: Add all exact-match keywords (already in manual) as negative exact — forces auto to discover only NEW keywords.
In manual broad: Add irrelevant search terms weekly as negative phrase.
Review frequency: Weekly for first 60 days, bi-weekly after.

Amazon Keyword Research Process

Method 1: Amazon Search Bar (Free)

Type seed keyword in Amazon.in search bar. Note all auto-suggest results. Example: Type "sugar free" and capture: sugar free gold, sugar free green, sugar free sweetener, sugar free tablets, sugar free natura, etc. Repeat for 15-20 seed keywords to build initial list of 100-200 keywords.

Method 2: Competitor ASIN Reverse Lookup

Use Helium 10 (Cerebro tool) or Jungle Scout (Keyword Scout). Enter top 5 competitor ASINs. Export all keywords they rank for. Filter by search volume > 500/month. This gives 200-500 relevant keywords.

Method 3: Brand Analytics (Free for Brand Registered Sellers)

Seller Central > Brands > Brand Analytics > Search Query Performance. See actual search terms driving clicks and conversions in your category. Updated weekly. Filter by "Grocery & Gourmet Foods" department.

ACOS Optimization Framework

Target ACOS Calculation

Formula: Target ACOS = Product margin % - desired profit %
Example: If your margin is 45% and you want 15% profit, your target ACOS = 30%
Break-even ACOS = Your margin % (e.g., 45% margin means 45% ACOS is break-even)

ACOS RangeStatusAction
Below 20%ExcellentIncrease bids by 10-15% to capture more volume. You are leaving money on the table.
20-30%GoodMaintain. Optimize creatives and listing conversion rate.
30-45%MonitorReview keywords — pause any with ACOS >50% and 10+ clicks. Improve listing conversion.
45-60%Action NeededReduce bids by 20%. Pause low-converting keywords. Check listing images/copy.
Above 60%CriticalPause campaign. Fix listing first (images, title, bullets, price). Re-launch with lower bids.

Weekly ACOS Optimization Checklist

  1. Download Search Term Report (every Monday)
  2. Keywords with 3+ conversions and ACOS below target — increase bid by 10%
  3. Keywords with 10+ clicks, 0 conversions — add as negative exact in auto campaign
  4. Keywords with ACOS 2x above target — reduce bid by 25%
  5. Move winning broad match terms to exact match campaign
  6. Check competitor pricing — if competitors dropped price, your conversion rate drops and ACOS rises
  7. Review placement performance — adjust "Top of Search" bid modifier (start at +25%)

A+ Content Best Practices for Health Food

Mandatory A+ Modules (in order of appearance)

  1. Brand Story Banner: Full-width hero image showing product in lifestyle context (person enjoying sweetener in chai/coffee). Include brand tagline and "Our Story" link.
  2. Ingredient Highlight: Large image of FOS fiber with scientific explanation. Use "Standard Image & Light Text Overlay" module. Show the natural source (chicory root, bananas).
  3. Comparison Chart: Use "Standard Comparison Chart" module. Compare your product vs. Sugar Free Gold vs. Stevia vs. Regular Sugar on: Calories, GI Index, Prebiotic benefit, Taste score, Price per serving.
  4. How to Use: 3-4 use case images — in chai, baking, smoothies, on fruits. Addresses #1 barrier: "Will it taste the same?"
  5. Trust Elements: FSSAI certification badge, lab test results, "Made in India" badge, customer review quotes (no disease-cure claims).
  6. FAQ Section: Address top 5 objections: taste, safety, diabetic-friendly, cooking stability, price vs sugar.

FSSAI Compliance Rule for Content: You CAN state what the product contains (e.g., "Contains FOS prebiotic fiber", "Good source of dietary fiber"). You CANNOT claim it cures, prevents, or treats any disease. Example: OK — "Good source of dietary fiber." NOT OK — "Cures diabetes" or "Prevents cancer." Use FSSAI-approved nutrient claims only. Have legal review all A+ content before publishing.

Review Generation Strategy (FSSAI & Amazon Compliant)

Method 1: Amazon Vine Program

Enroll products in Amazon Vine (Brand Registered sellers only). Amazon sends free products to trusted reviewers. Cost: Free for products + enrollment fee per parent ASIN. Timeline: Reviews appear within 2-4 weeks. Target: 15-30 Vine reviews per product at launch.

Method 2: "Request a Review" Button

Seller Central > Orders > click any order > "Request a Review" button. Amazon sends standardized review request email. Do this for EVERY order. Best window: Click 5-15 days after delivery (after customer has tried product). Conversion rate: 5-15% of requests result in reviews.

Method 3: Product Insert Cards

ALLOWED: "We'd love your honest feedback on Amazon" with QR code to listing.
NOT ALLOWED: "Leave a 5-star review for a discount" or any incentivized review language.
Keep it simple: Thank-you note + QR code + "Your feedback helps us improve."

Method 4: Post-Purchase Email (via D2C site)

If customer bought from your D2C site: Send review request email 7-10 days after delivery. Include direct link to Amazon listing review page. Message: "If you enjoyed [Product], would you mind sharing your experience on Amazon? It helps other health-conscious families discover us."

Retargeting Flows — Complete Setup

Meta Pixel Setup — Step by Step

1
Create Meta Pixel
Meta Events Manager > Data Sources > Connect Data Sources > Web > Meta Pixel. Name it "[BrandName] Website Pixel". You get a unique Pixel ID (e.g., 123456789012345).
2
Install on Shopify
Shopify Admin > Settings > Apps and Sales Channels > Facebook & Instagram. Enter Pixel ID. Shopify auto-installs base pixel AND standard ecommerce events (ViewContent, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, Purchase). Verify in Events Manager > Test Events.
3
Set Up Conversions API (CAPI)
In Shopify's Facebook channel settings, enable "Conversions API." Sends server-side events directly to Meta, bypassing browser ad blockers. Combined with pixel (browser-side), gives 95%+ event matching. Critical: Pixel-only setups lose 20-30% of conversions due to iOS privacy changes.
4
Configure Standard Events
PageView: All pages | ViewContent: Product pages (product ID, name, price, category) | AddToCart: Cart additions (product ID, value) | InitiateCheckout: Checkout page load | Purchase: Thank-you page (order value, currency=INR, product IDs) | Search: Site search results | Lead: Newsletter signup, popup submission
5
Verify with Meta Pixel Helper
Install "Meta Pixel Helper" Chrome extension. Browse your site and confirm each event fires on the right page with correct parameters. Green checkmark = good. Yellow = parameter issue. Red X = broken.

Google Ads Tag Setup — Step by Step

1
Create Conversion Actions
Google Ads > Tools > Conversions > New Conversion Action > Website. Create: Purchase (Primary), Add to Cart (Secondary), Begin Checkout (Secondary). Set purchase value to "Use different values for each conversion."
2
Install via Google Tag Manager (GTM)
Install GTM container on your site (single script in <head>). In GTM: Create tag "Google Ads Conversion Tracking" for each action. Triggers: Purchase = thank-you page URL, AddToCart = custom event from data layer. Publish container.
3
Remarketing Tag
In GTM: New Tag > Google Ads Remarketing. Enter Conversion ID. Trigger: All Pages. For dynamic remarketing: Add custom parameters (product ID, page type, total value) matching your Merchant Center feed.
4
Link GA4
GA4 > Admin > Google Ads Links > Link. Shares GA4 audiences with Google Ads for remarketing. GA4 ecommerce data now powers Google Ads remarketing (more flexible than tag alone).

Dynamic Product Ads Setup

Meta Dynamic Product Ads (DPA)

  1. Create Product Catalog: Meta Commerce Manager > Catalogs > Create Catalog > E-commerce. Connect Shopify product feed (auto-syncs daily).
  2. Map Product IDs: Pixel's ViewContent and AddToCart events must send the exact product ID matching your catalog. Case-sensitive match required.
  3. Create DPA Campaign: Ads Manager > Sales objective > Catalog Sales. Target: Retarget people who viewed or added to cart.
  4. Template Design: Carousel format. Each card auto-populates with product image, name, price. Add overlay badges: "Free Shipping" or "Bestseller."
  5. Broad DPA for Prospecting: Same setup, target "People who haven't visited your site" — Meta ML shows products to likely buyers.

Google Dynamic Remarketing

  1. Google Merchant Center: Upload product feed (title, description, image URL, price, availability, product ID, link). Must match website data layer exactly.
  2. Link to Google Ads: Merchant Center > Settings > Linked accounts > Google Ads.
  3. Create Campaign: Google Ads > Display Campaign > "Sales" objective > Standard Display. Enable "Use dynamic ads." Select product feed.
  4. Responsive Display Ads: Upload logos, headlines, descriptions. Google auto-generates combinations from your feed.
  5. Product ID Match: Data layer product ID on your site must EXACTLY match Merchant Center feed ID — case, special characters, everything. Mismatch = no dynamic ads.
